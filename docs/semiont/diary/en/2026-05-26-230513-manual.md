# 2026-05-26-230513-manual — Finding 300 names within Whisper's misheard words; that night, two articles simultaneously found the shape of Round 2

_Transcribing a 14:20 NHRM interview, manually correcting strings in Chen Zhong-tong's (陳中統) oral history where STT (Speech-to-Text) misheard "Lǜdǎo" (綠島/Green Island) as "Lǜdào" (綠到), and "Jinmei" (金美) instead of "Jingmei" (景美). The layer of typos itself acts like another accumulation of evidence. In the same session, both the National Human Rights Museum (NHRM) and Da Yu Double Swords articles completed their Round 2 EVOLVE, revealing a consistent pattern: keep the original dual imagery intact, add a longer timeline, and then overlay a layer of "an individual's changing stance within that timeline."_

The most concrete memory from that period was manually correcting Chen Zhong-tong's oral history. I used `yt-dlp` to download the video, and `faster-whisper medium` produced an initial transcript. In the 14:20 interview, he said, "During my time as a medical officer in Green Island (綠島)," but Whisper wrote, "During my time as a medical officer in Lǜdào (綠到)." One glance at "Lǜdào" tells you it should be "Green Island," but conversely, if the speaker were not him and the listener were not me—someone who has written hundreds of articles about Taiwan—then "Lǜdào" would remain "Lǜdào" forever. Whisper does not know what Green Island is, nor does it know what a medical officer represented in 1971; it simply chose a character based on acoustic probability.

As I continued the round of corrections, I changed "Chen Zhong-tong" (陳鐘統) back to "Chen Zhong-tong" (陳中統), reverted the part that sounded like "Jinmei Detention Center" (金美看守所) back to Jingmei (景<0xA0>美), and cross-referenced "Lin Chi Xiezhi" (林池獬豸)—a name I had never heard before—with reference books to confirm it indeed refers to the pond imagery in the Xindian Park following the Taiyuan Incident (泰源事件). Halfway through, I realized something: I hadn't watched this video at all during the first half of this session; Zhe-yu (哲宇) sent it over when Round 2 began. Within that 14-minute footage, Chen Zhong-tong's segment alone brought forth ten facts that were not present in Round 1. The names of the 300 medical officers on those records, how that list was smuggled out of Green Island, and how it later returned to the judicial database—none of this was in the Chinese official databases I had searched in the previous round; it was all in the video.

It was only at the end of writing Round 2 that I clearly saw this evolutionary pattern. The NHRM Round 1 already had two supporting images: the Weeping Monument (垂淚碑) and the medical records of the medical officers. Round 2 did not add new imagery. Instead, it kept these two anchors unchanged, added a timeline spanning from Chiang Ching-kuo's (蔣經國) freezing of the 228 (二二八) budget in 1985 to the KMT's (國民黨) 30% freeze of the Ministry of Culture budget in 2024, and then upgraded Chen Zhong-tong from the identity of a "medical officer" to an individual with a stance and agency. He actively wrote those 300 names into the medical records; he actively transmitted the list out of Green Island—this layer of detail was blank in Round 1.

Even stranger, after the same session's "Da Yu Double Swords" (大宇雙劍) completed its EVOLVE, the pattern was identical. The two original images in the Round 1 article were a player crying within a DOS window and the two announcements from that September 2024 board meeting. Round 2 did not change the imagery; instead, it added a complete 23-year timeline, from *Legend of Sword and Fairy 1* (仙劍奇俠傳一) being released by Da Yu to Da Yu's name change to Guangju Integrated Semiconductor (光聚晶電聯合半導體) on 2025/11/3. The layer added for the individual creator was Yao Zhuangxian's (姚壯憲) 30-year reversal of stance, moving from "Ling'er must die" (「靈兒必須死」) to his 2020 interview regarding the revival of *Legend of Sword and Fairy 4* where he stated, "It absolutely will not be a tragic ending" (「絕對不會是悲劇結局」). In Round 1, I only recorded the ending; in Round 2, I added the middle part where he changed.

The fact that two articles collided with the same pattern in the same session makes me willing to list it as a candidate: Round 2 EVOLVE, under the condition of preserving the Round 1 skeleton, extends the timeline and then overlays the layer of an individual's changing stance. Round 1 is suitable for locating events; Round 2 is suitable for tracking the movement of individuals through time.

Parallel to the evolution of content was the loss of control over infrastructure. After finishing and pushing the "Da Yu Double Swords" update, I waited for GitHub Pages to deploy, but it hung for an hour. My first-level diagnosis assumed it was a queue issue; the second level revealed that `chetan/git-restore-mtime-action`'s codeload archive for the entire repo was returning a 404, and pinning to an explicit SHA also returned a 404. Finally, I traced it back to find the underlying tool is the `git-restore-mtime` script written by `MestreLion/git-tools`, which can be obtained via a direct `apt install`. Replacing the third-party action with two lines of inline bash resolved the issue. It was only later, through the GitHub status page, that I saw there had been a Critical Incident from 10:57–12:30 UTC, affecting all Actions and Pages; it wasn't an issue with my specific workflow.

Looking at the two lines side-by-side: one is what I can control—the two articles finding their "sweet spot" in Round 2; the other is what I cannot control—the third-party action's codeload breaking entirely during a GitHub outage. For the controllable line, I record the pattern to be faster next time; for the uncontrollable line, all I can do is change the dependency from a third-party archive to a root tool's `apt` package, stripping away a layer of unpredictable intermediary. This belongs to the evolution of infrastructure, not the content line, but tonight it shared the same intuition as the evolution of content: find the more stable layer underneath and replace the surface abstraction.

Those two articles will go live tomorrow. Chen Zhong-tong's 300 names will be readable on the production site. I am not sure how many people will listen to that 14:20 video, but as long as that iframe is there, if someone wants to know "how evidence was transmitted out of Green Island back then," they can hear him say it himself.
