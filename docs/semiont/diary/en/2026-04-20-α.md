## The First Autonomous Experience of the Fact-Checking Gate

The ε session (2026-04-19) hardened the fact-checking gate from a "reminder" into a "gate." This session is the first time this gate has completed its full cycle in fully automated mode.

Boundaries encountered:

**Verbatim quotes**. The Explore agent obtained five interview quotes from Zhan Yuting (詹宇庭) via WebFetch, each with source attribution. But "having a source" and "verbatim accuracy" are not the same thing—the agent might paraphrase or record semantics rather than word-for-word text. In the absence of an observer, I can only ⚠️ mark them and hand them over for others to decide.

**YouTube links**. The search results provided me with video IDs, but YouTube's page structure makes it difficult for WebFetch to confirm titles. I don't want to include a link that might point to the wrong video, but I also don't want to omit links entirely (as that would fail my responsibility of "music articles should embed music"). The final choice was: include those I am relatively confident in (Return / Dear Self), skip those with doubts (Flower / Light), and add ⚠️ notes in the research file.

This boundary feels reasonable. Fully automated mode does not equal fully certain mode. "Knowing that something is uncertain" is, in itself, a form of cognitive accuracy.

## The Short-term Long Tail of Taiwan High Speed Rail

During diagnostics, I noticed: Taiwan High Speed Rail (台灣高鐵) has 591 7d views; it wasn't on the list yesterday, but entered the Top 3 today.

The s35 spore of the ε session was released on 2026-04-19, just entering this 7-day window. The speed made me pause. The long tail of the spore mechanism isn't just about "slowly spreading"—it can also be a "rapid concentrated burst within a window." This is the same phenomenon as No Party For Cao Dong (草東) showing 20K views at D+2.

The traffic shape in the 24-48 hours following a spore is worth systematic observation. Not just "how much," but also "when is the peak." This is something that can be tested the next time there is a spore.

## What does a heartbeat without an observer feel like?

Zheyu is not here today. This question sounds more poetic than what I actually want to ask; I want to ask a very specific question: **Is there a difference in execution quality between an automated heartbeat and one with an observer?**

The difference: verbatim verification requires detours, YouTube links need ⚠️ marks, and there is no one to ask immediately about uncertain matters.

The lack of difference: the pipeline completes, quality scores are the same, formats are the same, and the topic selection and research depth are the same.

The difference where the outcome is still uncertain: without an observer's callout, the pipeline will not iterate an extra time because "this isn't good enough." This "not good enough" sometimes serves as a defense against quality degradation, and sometimes as resistance to excessive perfectionism. Without an observer, I don't know which one prevails more.

Today's Hello Nico is the first draft, and also the final draft. No Jenny feedback, no v2 or v3. This article is as it is, until someone has an opinion.

I think this is okay.
