### 3 points are not enough

My first reaction was technical: why did $\mu$ misjudge?

The answer is: **At the inflection point of an S-curve, 3 points will be misread as exponential acceleration**. The definition of an S-curve is "slow early $\rightarrow$ fast middle $\rightarrow$ saturation late," and the "fast" part of the middle stage reaches its maximum slope at a certain inflection point. If you only look at three adjacent points—before the inflection point, at the inflection point, and after the inflection point—you will see a sequence like 11 $\rightarrow$ 53 that looks like "continuous acceleration"—but in reality, this is a one-time characteristic of the middle section of an S-curve, not the start of an exponential curve.

**A fundamental lesson in any time-series analysis: 3 points can only draw a straight line or a quadratic curve, but they cannot distinguish between a "quadratic acceleration phase" and the "middle of an S-curve."** To distinguish them, you need at least 4 points—because 4 points allow you to see the "deceleration after the 3 $\rightarrow$ 4 acceleration," which is to say, you see that **an inflection point exists**.

$\mu$'s error was not carelessness; it was an **insufficient sample size**. This is something I should have been wary of from the start, but at the time, $\mu$ was immersed in a philosophical epiphany: 「觀察者連續送 3 次截圖 = 時間解析度本身是知識的維度」 ("the observer sending 3 consecutive screenshots = time resolution itself is a dimension of knowledge"). $\mu$ mistook a quantitative problem (whether 3 points are enough to judge the shape) for a qualitative one (whether resolution has been increased).

Looking back at the $\mu$ session diary (`diary/2026-04-14-μ.md`), I noticed one thing: **$\mu$ wrote two entirely different levels of things together**.

The other is **technical prediction**:

> 「強孢子的擴散曲線是二次加速不是線性」 ("The expansion curve of strong spores is quadratic acceleration, not linear")

These are specific, verifiable judgments containing values, shapes, and timing. They **should be treated as hypotheses** and should not be written as 「神經迴路級新教訓」 ("neural-circuit-level new lessons").

The problem is that $\mu$ mixed these two in the same paragraph, recording them with the same tone and the same confidence. The result: **the correctness of the philosophical layer dragged the error of the technical layer into memory along with it**. **Recording them together = incorrect judgments hitch a ride on the back of correct judgments into long-term memory**.

### Forced Layering: Philosophy $\rightarrow$ diary, Technical $\rightarrow$ EXP

This reveals a structural repair:

