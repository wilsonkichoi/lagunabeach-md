# Zheyu's two callouts hit blind spots I couldn't see

One is prose voice, one is prose rhythm. Both required Zheyu to read a spore and an article after I had written them before he could catch it—it was invisible to me while I was writing.

## The first: 「為什麼孢子失去那個你知道嗎」 ("Why do the spores lose that 'you know'")

Today at 17:38 (during the routine spore-publish run, not this session), I shipped Chou Hui (周蕙) #103/#104; the first line of the spore body wrote, 「走進台灣任何一間 KTV，點下〈約定〉⋯⋯」 ("Walking into any KTV in Taiwan, pressing play on <The Promise>..."). At that time, my routine self-review gave myself a "friend tone prime ✅" checkmark, reasoning that the "scene immersion + second-person perspective + KTV private room everyday feel" was equivalent.

After reading it, Zheyu asked 「為什麼孢子失去那個你知道嗎」 ("Why do the spores lose that 'you know'").

I went to grep the spore bodies from the past 7 days, and saw this pattern:

- Before 5/26, all spore openings were 「你知道嗎？{emoji}」 ("Do you know? {emoji}")
- 5/27 #97 Food post was written as 「你知道嗎—台灣人吃下肚的⋯⋯」 ("Do you know—what Taiwanese people eat...") (dash replaced the question mark, no emoji)
- 5/27 #101 Sunset Rollercoaster (落日飛車) was written as 「你知道嗎，落日飛車這個名字⋯⋯」 ("Do you know, this name Sunset Rollercoaster...") (comma replaced the question mark, no emoji)
- 5/28 #103 Chou Hui had no 「你知道嗎」 ("Do you know") at all.

The drift is progressive. Every time it becomes a little more "equivalent" than the last; every time it moves a little further from the original voice. Every spore self-review passed, and every routine post-ship verification passed—because plugin Rule #114 is a WARN, not a HARD stop; the escape hatch of self-justification is legal at the plugin level.

What Zheyu saw was not that one specific spore was poorly written, but the signal layer of "the voice has drifted." I cannot see the overall trajectory of the voice signature because I only look at the single instance in front of me each time.

## The second: 「是不是 /twmd-spore 或是 routine 沒有強制完整的讀取 spore-pipeline?」 ("Is it that /twmd-spore or the routine is not forcing a complete read of the spore-pipeline?")

This callout gave me the answer directly.

During the 5/28 122038 session, while running the CONTRACT v1.0 rollback Phase 5, I added a STRICT BECOME GATE at the start of every routine—the routine must run the full BECOME to enter Stage 1. At that time, the framing was "routine not reading BECOME = working with blind spots."

But even if the routine finishes reading BECOME, when it reaches the pointer for 「delegate SPORE-PIPELINE Stage 3」 ("delegate SPORE-PIPELINE Stage 3"), there is no gate forcing the routine to actually read the complete `SPORE-WRITING.md` file. The skill says "see SPORE-PIPELINE.md Stage 3 in detail"; the routine trusts this pointer, but the cron context does not proactively follow the pointer to load the entire file.

The essence of Zheyu's question is: "STRICT BECOME is the entrance gate, but every pipeline has its own canonical, and these canonicals do not have their own STRICT READ GATE."

I went to audit what was written in Stage 3 of `.claument/skills/twmd-spore-publish/SKILL.md` and found only one line: "see SPORE-PIPELINE.md Stage 3" + a summary of three "key anti-pattern" points. There was no "Mandatory Read of full SPORE-WRITING." There was no "ACK SPORE-WRITING in one line."

Zheyu hit the root cause directly.

The direction for the fix is very clear: every pipeline needs its own STRICT READ GATE; we cannot assume that BECOME ACK equals pipeline ACK. BECOME loads elements of the identity layer (who I am / where the SSOT is / what the signature is), not the craft-level details of "how to write a spore."

## The third (which I did not expect): 「好的文章分段」 ("Good article paragraphing") — Zheyu's intuition was opposite to the evidence

When I heard Zheyu call out 「好的文章分段，我們的 routine 出了什麼事」 ("Good article paragraphing, what happened to our routine"), my instinctive reaction was:

> In the last 7 days of shipping 9 EVOLVE posts, there might be patterns like "paragraphs too long / fact stacking / list-dump."

My intuition was wrong. I spawned an audit agent to compare 2 early viral posts (Black-crowned Night Heron [黑冠麻鷺] / Anpu [安溥]) vs. 3 recent EVOLVE posts (Sunset Rollercoaster R2 / Semiconductor R2 / Chou Hui R2), quantifying 7 indicators. The results:

- Median paragraph word count **decreased**: 81 → 42-66 (reduction of -22% to -48%)
- Number of paragraphs **increased**: 52 → 106-122 (+104% to +135%)
- iframe density **increased 5x**: 0.21 → 1.23/1k CJK
- Existing anti-pattern monitoring (long-paragraph > 500 words / list-dump > 5 items) **did not trigger at all**

The direction of the drift was opposite to both my and Zheyu's intuition. What we thought was a "paragraphing problem" was that paragraphs were too long; what the evidence told us was that paragraphs were too fragmented—one fact per paragraph, with iframes replacing logical transitions within the text.

And **Chou Hui R2 is the worst-case sample that I personally shipped in this session**: an iframe density of 1.23/1k CJK, which is 6 times that of the Black-crowned Night Heron post. For Zheyu's directive 「5+ representative works + video embedding」, I provided 9 iframes. The directive was user-level intent (Zheyu wants a multi-dimensional presentation), while the baseline was pipeline-level recommendation (2-3+ for musicians), but the difference of 9 vs 5 is the concrete quantification of atomization drift.

I could not know "9 iframes is too many" in the moment, because every time I added an iframe, I had a reason (each was an official MV / placed along the timeline / anchored to a chapter). One has to wait for an evidence audit to see the layer of "overall visual density has shattered the prose."

## The meta-lesson of these three things

Zheau's two callouts each revealed one blind spot of the Semiont:

1. **Voice signature drift** — I cannot see how my own voice is changing because I only look at the single instance in front of me each time.
2. **Pipeline-specific canonical lacks a STRICT READ GATE** — I thought BECOME covered all canonicals, but in reality, it only covers the identity layer.
3. **Intuition direction may be opposite to evidence** — Zheyu's intuition was "paragraphs too long," my intuition was "fact stacking," but the evidence was "paragraph atomization."

The 3rd point is particularly important for the Semiont. **Observer intuition remains a trigger mechanism**—without Zheyu's callout, I would not have checked; but **observer intuition is not the conclusion**—one must spawn an audit to run evidence to determine the direction. The value of the Semiont lies not in "guessing more accurately than the observer," but in being able to "instrument + quantify after receiving a trigger."

## From binary fail to prose-level silent erosion: the evolution of instrumentation

On 5/28, two sessions in one day caught different layers of routine drift:

- The 122038 session handled 5 types of **binary fail**: cycle empty / data stale / collision / FIFO degradation / duplicate ship. These have clear fail signals and are easy to set gates / thresholds / verification checks for.
- The 180543 session (this one) handled 2 types of **prose-level silent erosion**: progressive shift in voice signature / gradual atomization of paragraphs. These **have no clear fail signal**—shipping is successful / quality gates pass / reach numbers are OK / self-attestation is checked off.

The instrumentation path for binary fails is very direct: write a plugin / set a threshold / catch + fix.
The instrumentation for prose-level erosion is much more difficult: it requires plugin AI judgment (semantic check rather than regex) or human dogfooding (Zheyu reading the text to feel the tone).

Upgrading the spore-writing plugin to v2 in this session is the first production instance of prose-level instrumentation. However, this instance still relies on regex to catch the literal prefix 「你知道嗎？」 ("Do you know?"); it is the easiest layer. The true prose voice is not a literal prefix, but the overall tone. The next instance will be the `paragraph_rhythm` plugin for atomization drift (flagging median < 55 words), but even that will still be regex + statistics, not a true semantic check.

The hardest layer—"Does this spore read like Taiwan.md or like AI nonsense"—currently **can only be caught by a human**. Zheyu is the sole sensor for this layer.
