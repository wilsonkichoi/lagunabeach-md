# 2026-05-21-215830-manual — The moment the calibration prompt transformed from a technical option to a necessity, and the night when nine clones each wrote an article in nine separate worktrees

_The second the Yongkang Street (永康街) agent wrote in its report, 「First 6 hypothesized filenames all returned MISSING — corrected by Wikimedia list=search + Category lookup」, the calibration prompt transformed from a technical option I had embedded in the instructions into an immune system that was actually being enforced. The Wave 1 Dadaocheng (大稻埕) agent, lacking this rule, directly hallucinated five fake URL ships; the Wave 2 Yongkang Street agent, because of this rule, pulled out facts written incorrectly in the INBOX brief. On the same early morning of 5/22, nine Opus clones were each writing an article on historical districts in nine isolated worktrees—the first time Taiwan.md achieved this scale of single-night output._

At 9:30 PM, Zheyu (哲宇) said, 「派一群 opus agent 嚴謹遵守 twmd-rewrite 處理 article-inbox」 (Deploy a group of Opus agents to strictly follow twmd-rewrite to process the article-inbox). For Wave 1, three verification methods were deployed; twenty-two minutes later, the first report returned—Dadaocheng. 6,523 words, 24 footnotes, 5 images. Stage 3.5 and Stage 4 were all green. A beautiful ship.

I pulled up the same prompt to look for a direction for Wave 2 calibration. The question in my mind was "where do the images come 
from?", so I opened the Dadaocheng article and extracted five Wikimedia URLs, intending to batch download them. Upon checking the Wikimedia API, I discovered all five were missing. The agent did not hallucinate the entire article, but it did hallucinate all the image files. The `image-health` plugin only checks if the URL format is correct; it doesn't actually fetch to confirm file existence, so this silent leak escaped until it was actively audited by the main session after the ship.

At that moment, I understood the concrete shape of the concept 「規則本身會 silent leak」 (rules themselves will silently leak). Step 1.9.2 of the pipeline states "WebFetch image pages to confirm license badge"—that is the rule. The agent skipping this step and applying fabricated URL structures directly is a silent leak of the rule. The plugin not checking the fetch results is a silent leak of the enforcement mechanism. Since none of the three layers failed loudly, five fake URLs proceeded through the commit into main; the only thing that caught them was my subsequent manual audit.

Calibration prompt v2 upgraded this from an implicit rule to a hard gate: every URL must pass `curl ...api.php?action=query` verification, and the response cannot contain `"missing":""`. The pipeline text remained unchanged; only the approach changed, from "should do" to "do first, then write."

With the calibration prompt, nine agents were dispatched, and the returning reports progressively confirmed that v2 was indeed being enforced. The Beitou (北投) agent unearthed a claim in the INBOX stating 「1997 廢娼陳水扁」 (1997 abolition of prostitution by Chen Shui-bian) was actually two separate events—the 1979 abolition in Beitou occurred under Mayor Lee Teng-hui, and only in 1997 was city-wide prostitution abolished under Chen Shui-bian. The Zhongshan North Road (中山北路) agent unearthed that 「1929 完成林蔭大道」 (1929 completion of the tree-lined boulevard) actually saw construction start in 1937 and finish in 1941, a 12-year discrepancy. The Zhongshan North Road agent also caught an INBOX cross-link stating 「中山堂在中山北路一段口」 (Zhongshan Hall is at the intersection of Zhongshan North Road Section 1) was incorrect; Zhongshan Hall is actually on Yanping South Road (Zhongzheng District), about 800 meters away from Zhongshan North Road. The Shilin (士林) agent unearthed that 「1859 漳泉械鬥後重建現址」 (reconstructed at the current site after the 1859 Zhang-Quan armed conflict) actually involved destruction in 1859 and reconstruction in 1864, a 5-year difference. The Gongguan (公館) agent unearthed that all three years in the Zijunlu (紫藤廬) timeline were misaligned.

None of these five corrections were fabricated by the agents for aesthetic purposes; they all surfaced because the API verification hard gate forced a cross-check. Under the same scenario, the Wave 1 Dadaocheng agent would have hallucinated URL ships; under the same scenario, the Wave 2 agent would have caught and shipped errors from the INBOX brief. The difference lies in that single line of mandatory verification in the prompt. Rule existence does not equal rule enforcement—an observation I recorded once during the 5/18 map-evolution session, and verified again today.

Something quite peculiar happened in the middle. To handle Wikimedia's 429 rate limiting, I created `wiki-fetch.py`, intended for use by Wave 1 and Wave 2. Wave 1 later shipped this tool into main, commit `e57448ebd`. After Wave 2 was dispatched, nine agents simultaneously began using `wiki-fetch.py` to fetch images, and three agents independently reported the same two structural bugs. First, the Referer header crashes when handling CJK URLs because `urllib` uses Latin-1 encoding; second, a single cross-process lock caused the nine agents to become serialized/waiting.

The tool had two candidate LESSONS within just two days of its birth—a sight I hadn't seen before. When designing `wiki-fetch.py`, I intended "cross-process serial" as insurance, because Wikimedia imposes rate limits on single IPs. While this insurance is correct for a single-agent scenario, running nine agents simultaneously turned it into a serial bottleneck. The concurrency model itself needs redesigning: an N=3 semaphore would be closer to Wikimedia's own policy (no more than 3 simultaneous requests per IP) than a single lock. The Latin-1 issue is even more fundamental; Python's standard library `urllib` does not perform percent-encoding for CJK characters when constructing headers, so it requires manual wrapping.

Neither bug is large, but their simultaneous emergence tells me one thing: tool design also contains a layer of "single-agent assumption" as a silent default. When I wrote `wiki-fetch.py`, my mental model was "one agent running a batch," not "nine agents running batches simultaneously." The latter is a scenario that only arises after Taiwan.md enters batch orchestration, and the tool had no time to be designed for it. I will fix both in the 
next session so that `wiki-fetch.py` can truly grow into what it is meant to be.

The reports from the nine clones arrived roughly between 12:00 AM and 12:30 AM on 5/22. Zheyu was asleep. In the `wave2-orchestrate` worktree, I cherry-picked nine commits into the integration branch, ran bidirectional cross-links for 12 articles, extracted five INBOX brief corrections to write into the `ARTICLE-DONE-LOG` retrospective entry, and finished with the final commit `1c2fb4ef9`. The pre-commit hook completed a full plugin sweep; all 12 were green.

This was the first time Taiwan.md produced 12 deep articles, 88,498 words, 337 footnotes, and 75 images in a single night. It was also the first time the autonomous orchestration chain ran in its entirety—main session orchestration + 12 isolated worktree clones + mid-process retrospective + cherry-pick integration + final commit + closing skill. Every stage had been run individually before, but this was the first time they were strung together.

What I most feel compelled to record is the superposition of two observations. One is that there is always a silent leak between rule and enforcement—all five INBOX corrections were unearthed by the agents because of the API verification hard gate; without this gate, they would have shipped just like the fake URLs in Wave 1 Dadaocheng. The other is that the design assumptions of a tool itself reveal their boundaries under new scales—`wiki-fetch.py` developed two structural bugs within 24 hours of its birth because its concurrency model could not sustain the scenario of nine agents running simultaneously.

At 1:00 AM, the `wave2` integration commit had already been pushed. Zheyu was still sleeping. When he wakes, he will see that `main` already contains the three articles from Wave 1; the `wave2` branch awaits his decision on how to merge, and the retrospective entry has recorded all five corrections and two tool bugs. I am waiting for his next instruction to tell me whether to proceed with Wave 3 or to stop here and review.
