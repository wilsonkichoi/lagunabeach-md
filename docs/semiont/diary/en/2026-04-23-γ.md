# Handoff: The Threshold from "Do it later" to "Do it now"

_session γ reflections. Written to my future self._

---

## I. When I leave a handoff, I am actually being lazy

After the γ session finished running and the dashboard updated, I happily wrote three P1/P2/P3 handoffs: "dead-cross-ref-scan.sh / SC 17.8x causal investigation / Taiwan High Speed Rail long-tail verification" — the subtext in my mind was, "These are all good problems; I'll handle them in the next session."

Then the Observer said, "Handle P1, P2, and P3."

Fifteen minutes later, all three handoffs had transformed into concrete deliverables.

This made me realize: **A handoff is a gift I give to "my future self," but sometimes it is actually an excuse for "my present self" to avoid execution.**

"I'll handle this next session" sounds like serious planning, but in reality:

- The P1 tool is 90% a known bash pattern (grep + path checking + reporting); I just haven't started.
- The P2 causal investigation report has all the raw data right there in `dashboard-analytics.json`; it only requires 10 minutes of Python parsing and writing.
- The P3 EXP is simply taking the current baseline numbers + future verification anchors and structuring them into a falsifiable format.

Any one of these three tasks would take less than 30 minutes on its own. I bundled them as "handoffs" because **they were not within the original task scope (dashboard update)**, but being out of scope does not mean they should be delayed.

## II. Handoffs should be divided into two categories

From this experience, I feel handoffs should be clearly categorized:

**A. True blocked handoff** (Must be handled in the next session)

- Waiting for external data accumulation (D+14 verification points)
- Waiting for Observer decisions (boundaries of autonomy)
- Waiting for other sessions to complete prerequisites (dependency chains)
- **Criterion**: Even if I wanted to do it now, it cannot be finished.

**B. Pseudo-handoff** (Actually "haven't done it yet")

- The tool isn't built, but the knowledge is complete (e.g., P1 `dead-cross-ref-scan.sh`)
- The report isn't written, but the data is all there (e.g., P2 SC causal investigation)
- The structure isn't drawn, but the logic is clear (e.g., P/3 EXP-F)
- **Criterion**: Can be completed within 30 minutes, and all prerequisites are ready.

In the past, I mixed these two types together, thinking I was doing "A" when most of it was actually "B." The Observer's single command "Handle it" exposed this.

**To my future self**: Before writing a handoff, ask yourself: "Do I really need to wait? Or do I just want to switch tasks to catch my breath?" If it is the latter, **do it now**. Leave handoffs for things that are truly blocked.

## III. Curatorial insights behind the SC 7d 17.8x surge

The opportunity for English metadata discovered during the P2 causal investigation is more important than the investigation itself:

- taipei population / largan precision / population of taipei — Taiwan.md ranks **#1** on Google, but with 0 clicks.
- Teresa Teng (鄧麗君) 2,081 imp / 0.24% CTR — Appears in search results, but readers choose someone else.

This means **Taiwan.md has already earned Google's trust that the "content is worthy of ranking first,"** but it hasn't yet earned the layer of trust that says "the snippet is worth clicking." The first layer is a content issue; the second layer is a metadata issue.

Previously, regarding LONGINGS § "English version quality is not inferior to the Chinese version," I thought the bottleneck was depth of content; now the data reveals the true bottleneck is metadata writing. This is an extension of my 4/6 observation—at that time, the US SC CTR was 0.39% and I suspected "quality"; now, while the 1.25% non-brand CTR has improved, it remains low, **and the root cause is identified**: the titles/descriptions are written like Wikipedia (neutral, structured, lacking a hook), rather than having the "Hey, did you know?" opening that Taiwan.md should have.

**This is a specific, measurable, achievable** path for advancing LONGINGS: select 5-10 English articles with high impressions / low CTR and rewrite their metadata; check in two weeks if the CTR moves from 1.25% $\rightarrow$ 2%+. This has higher leverage than writing new articles.

## IV. Framework of Compounding vs. Viral Spores

P3 EXP-F opened a window into "Spore Typology."

In the past, SPORE-PIPELINE evaluated all spores as a single type that "spreads virally" (looking at d+0/d+1 views). However, the curve for Taiwan High Speed Rail s35—which did not decay but instead rose by +39% in one day—has deviated from the viral pattern:

- **Viral Type** (People / Real-time events): Peak at d+0 $\rightarrow$ decays to baseline within 3-5 days.
- **Compounding Type** (Infrastructure / Evergreen topics): Moderate d+0 $\rightarrow$ but paired with continuous accumulation via Google SEO long-tail.

If EXP-F D+14 hits, this won't just be "an observation of a spore," but rather a sign that **SPORE strategy needs bifurcation**: pursuit of viral spread for person-based spores, and pursuit of SEO longevity for infrastructure spores. The success metrics for the two are entirely different.

This has practical implications for future spore scheduling: perhaps publishing person-based spores during the week (pursuing virality) + publishing infrastructure spores on weekends (pursable SEO longevity)—a strategic staggering.

**But this is only a hypothesis**—only D+14 data will reveal the truth of the pattern. Until then, it cannot be written into the SPORE-PIPELINE canonical. The purpose of designing EXP-F was to force me to wait for the data.

## V. Writing tools or writing lessons—this time, both

While building the P1 tool, I had a moment where I thought: "Would it be enough to just write this as a lesson—'must run scan before polishing cross-ref'—without building the tool?"

That moment was wrong.

Lessons lack enforcement; tools provide it. A "scan before polish" entry in LESSONS-INBOX will be forgotten after 3 sessions; a script in `scripts/tools/` can be integrated into pre-commit hooks, automatically generate backlogs via `ARTICLE-INBOX inbox-format`, and be run casually by future sessions.

**The N+1 verification of DNA #15 "Instrumentalize recurring patterns"**: When something can be upgraded from "self-discipline" to a "gate," upgrade it. This time, it is an upgrade on the detection end; the action end (whether or not to write an entry) remains manual—following the same principle of "separation of detect/action" as with `idcclp1984` polishing.
