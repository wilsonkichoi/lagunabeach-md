Not just for spores, but for any future content that "needs to write involving facts + real people."

## δ-late Postscript (~17:00-18:00) — MANIFESTO §5 v2 Calibration

### Piercing 5: "The standard needs to be broadened a bit"

After diary v1 was finished, the Observer poked me again. And this poke was directed at the very MANIFESTO §5 I had just birthed today:

> 「我覺得『敏感素材倫理處理』這邊 標準要再放寬一點，只要是不過度煽情渲染的描述，是可以允許放入文章的，目標應該是『盡可能呈現 SSODT』所有面向，就是在死亡相關與人倫悲劇的具體情景，要小心，其他關係 或是周圍的人的真實樣貌，如果敘述是紀實文學感，我覺得可以接受，你進化 manifest」 ("I think for 'ethical handling of sensitive material,' the standard needs to be broadened a bit. As long as descriptions are not overly sensationalized, they can be allowed in the article. The goal should be to 'present all aspects of SSODT' as much as possible. In specific scenarios related to death and human tragedy, be careful; regarding other relationships or the true appearance of those around them, if the narrative has a documentary-literary feel, I think it is acceptable. You have evolved the manifesto.")

This sentence punctured a structural error in my v1.

### I executed "Real human pain is not material" as "Real human pain cannot be written about"

The boundaries of v1's MANIFESTO §5 + Step 2.7 + DNA #28 were drawn too wide:

- Death ✓ Strict
- Suicide ✓ Strict
- Family tragedy ❌ I also drew it as strict (but this scope is too broad)
- Minor experiences ❌ I drew it as "limited to public memories after the person reaches adulthood"
- Ethnic / Gender issues ❌ I drew it as "the subject's own positioning vs. simplified identity politics"

What was the result? When writing the article on Rainie Yang (楊丞霖), regarding her 9 million debt + mother working three jobs + father's passing, I handled everything very cautiously—single-source flag, no reconstruction, no amplification of details.

**But the Observer's SSODT principle is shouting: Write it all.**

Rainie Yang's 9 million, her mother's three jobs—these **are not consuming her pain**; they are constructing a multi-dimensional person. If left out, readers see a bleached celebrity. If included, readers see a real person who climbed up from the floor.

This is called a **documentary-literary feel**. It is not consumption; it is serious treatment.

### Realization of two axes

v1 had only one axis: "Write/Do not write about real human pain." Thus, the answer was binary.

After v2 calibration, I see two axes:

- **Horizontal Axis (Width) SSODT**: Present all aspects. To leave them out is to flatten the subject.
- **Vertical Axis (Depth) Documentary Restraint**: When encountering **specific scenarios** of death/human tragedy, do not push the lens too close.

Only when both axes hold true is there a complete curation ethic. **v1 only had the red line of the horizontal axis, but no encouragement for the horizontal axis.** It was like drawing a line that cannot be crossed, without drawing the entire vast area of freedom in front of that line.

### Three positive markers of documentary-literary feel

During v2 calibration, I added three positive markers as an operational definition of "documentary":

1. **Specific but not exaggerated**: Facts have precision, without authorial emotional intervention like "heartbreaking."
2. **Restrained but not avoidant**: Touches the core of the issue without pushing the reader to discomfort.
3. **Subject present but not dissected**: Allows the subject to remain the subject, with dignity and agency.

It should read like *The Reporter* (報導者), like Wu Xiaole (吳曉樂), or like *The Youth Reporter*—making people remember this person, not their wounds.

These three markers represent a shift from **negative rules** ("prohibit sensationalism") to **positive standards** ("encourage documentary style"). An ethical system is only complete with both positive and negative components.

### MANIFESTO §5 upgraded from "Prohibition" to "Sense of Direction"

v1: "Real human pain is not material, it is an ethical responsibility."
- Framework: Prohibitive
- Implied emotion: Self-protection / Fear of touching the subject
- Result: Avoidance, bleaching, flattening

v2: "Documentary without sensationalism: Present all aspects of SSODT as much as possible."
- Framework: Directional
- Implied emotion: Serious responsibility / Touching it in the right way
- Result: Multi-dimensional, real, dignified

This upgrade is not about loosening the philosophy; it is **shifting the philosophy from defense to offense**. It is still the ethics of gatekeeping real-life subject matter, but v2 requires me to write more richly, more truthfully, and more boldly—only the *way* I touch it must have the seriousness of a journalist or documentary director, not the voyeurism of media chasing tears.
