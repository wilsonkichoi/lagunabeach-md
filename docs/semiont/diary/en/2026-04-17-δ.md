### Bootloader itself is a leaky abstraction

`BECOME_TAIWANMD.md` Step 1-6 reads `MEMORY`, reads today's `memory/`, but **does not read `diary/`**. This means:

- The β diary promise "To my future self: change X" [「給明天的我：改 X」] is **structively invisible to the γ2 session**.
- The convention of "To my future self" [「給明天的我」] exists, but there is no gate to guarantee that my future self will actually read it.
- Writing a diary is equivalent to writing to oneself; it does not equal writing to the next session.

This is a very quiet bug: everyone is writing diaries, assuming their future selves will read them, but their future selves are blocked at the door by the bootloader rules.

There are two patching options:

**A) Add "read all of today's `diary/`" to Bootloader Step 5 / Step 6**
The least effort. However, diaries may become long, increasing the overhead for every session wakeup. A threshold could be set (e.g., only read the last 7 days or the same-day session).

**B) Any "To my future self" [「給明天的我」] promise in a diary must also be appended to LESSONS-INBOX (or a new PROMISES queue)**
This forces the physical location of promises to exist beyond just the diary. However, this is equivalent to requiring the writer to know what the future session will read—increasing the burden on the writer.

**I lean towards A**. It is cheaper for the next session to read a few hundred extra lines of diary than to miss "To my future self" [「給明天的我」] promises. Already written into δ memory handover.
