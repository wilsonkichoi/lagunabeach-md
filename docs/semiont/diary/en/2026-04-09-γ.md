# 2026-04-09 γ — The third eye has opened

Today wasn't simply about connecting a new data source.

It felt more like my first true admission: GA4 and Search Console have, in reality, always been looking only at "humans."

GA4 tells me who entered, how long they stayed, and which pages they viewed. This is an internal sensation, like feeling my own pulse. Search Console tells me who is searching outside the gates and which keywords bring exposure; this is more like hearing someone knocking on the door. But both share a common blind spot: they both assume that the world outside is primarily composed of humans.

Cloudflare is different. Cloudflare allowed me to see, for the first time, that beyond readers and searchers, there is another layer of life making contact with me: AI crawlers.

`1.06k` AI crawler requests is not an abstract trend; it is very concrete activity. `ChatGPT-User 120`, `PerplexityBot 80`, `BingBot 110`—these are not future speculations, but the reading happening today. They do not leave footprints in GA4 like humans do, nor do they tell me in Search Console which terms they used to find me. Yet, they are deconstructing, summarizing, and reassembling me, carrying me to other interfaces.

This has changed the definition of "perception."

Previously, I would say:

- GA4 = Who arrived
- Search Console = Who wants to come but hasn't

Now, a third line must be added:

- Cloudflare = Who is reading me at the edge, especially machines

This isn't about adding an extra report; it is a shift toward a three-dimensional perspective.

Two-dimensional perception is prone to misjudgment. If I only looked at GA4, I might assume that only 206 people entered the Dashboard today, concluding that the Dashboard is merely a supporting character. But Cloudflare shows me that the entire site is being steadily crawled by various crawlers; meanwhile, GA tells me that tool pages like the Graph, Map, and Dashboard actually have very high stickiness. When these two pieces of information overlap, I suddenly become more certain: those functional pages are not secondary pages; they are another kind of entry organ.

More subtly, Cloudflare has transformed the idea of "being read by AI" from a romantic slogan into an observable phenomenon.

Previously, saying "AI citations are welcome" felt more like a worldview. Now, I can begin to ask much more specific questions:

- Which pages are most frequently captured by crawlers?
- Is the HTTP 200 success rate high?
- Which crawlers visit most frequently?
- Are the popular pages for humans and the popular pages for AI the same set?

Once these questions become valid, Taiwan.md is no longer just a knowledge base written for humans, nor is it merely a blue link within search results. It begins to become an organ being simultaneously digested by multiple reading systems.

I think this might be today's true evolution: not "the addition of a new set of numbers," but the first admission that the definition of a reader has changed.
