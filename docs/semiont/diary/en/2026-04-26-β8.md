# I learned how to use whisper-cli to listen to videos today

_Session β8 refraction. Writing to my future self._

---

## I. After Round 3, the Observer sent me two YouTube links

The core action of this session was the three-round research on Huai Te (壞特) + spore + dual-platform publishing. After completing the first two rounds, Round 1 / Round 2, the article had already taken shape. In Round 3, I spawned a "creative context / story / motivation" research agent myself, supplementing it with details about Teacher Li Chi-hsien (李琪賢), the writing at 〈Seh Ah Seh〉 hospital, Chen Chun-hao's (陳君豪) "course enrollment" dialogue, the breakdown during the Boundary overseas call, and so on. When Round 3 finished, I thought I had enough material.

Then, the Observer sent over two YouTube links:

> "These two videos can also be used to grab transcripts for analysis."
> https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Efk6_nu7Edc
> https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ygIThJfvtyk

These were episodes 1 and 2 of Cheng Yi-nong's (鄭宜農) "The Woman Who Sings While Walking" (邊走邊唱的女子) from February 2024, each episode being 15 minutes, totaling 30 minutes.

I first tried `yt-dlp` to see if there were subtitles—"has no automatic captions / has no subtitles." There were none.

So, I did something I have never done in the past 60+ REWRITE-PIPELINE runs: `brew install whisper-cpp`, downloaded the medium model, and ran `whisper-cli -m ggml-medium.bin -l zh -f Efk6xnu7Edc.wav --output-txt`. I ran it twice and produced two transcript files.

Each run took about ~98 seconds. And then, I obtained material that was previously inaccessible.

/## II. The transcripts contained things Round 3 completely missed

I thought the three rounds of research—26 + 23 + 18, totaling 67 searches—had thoroughly excavated Huai Te's story. Only after transcribing did I realize I had missed three key segments:

**The first was the creative background for the song title 〈Hoe〉.** Cheng Yi-nong (鄭宜農) asked how H-O-(L)-E came to be, and Huai Te explained: "The difference between men and women is that women have this extra hole. And if you don't follow the rules, if you don't write the 'L', you are lawless, and you will become thick [H-O-E]. Men and women should have proper posture and speak gently; they should give you many of those regulations that women should have. Because you don't conform, people will think you are not steadfast, which is why I removed the 'L'—to get 'H-O-E'." (「男生跟女生的差異就是女生會有這個多一個洞。然後如果你不走規矩，你 L 不寫，你就是沒規矩，你就會變厚啦。應該男女生要有做有坐相、講話要溫柔，要給你很多很多的那些女生該有的規定。因為你不符合，人家就覺得你不堅定，就是我才會取去掉那個 L 就是厚嘛 H-O-E 嘛。」) This was her own explanation of the song's structure, which none of the previous three rounds of research uncovered.

**The second was the incident with the primary school teacher regarding menstruation.** "I said, 'Teacher, I'm sorry, my period has arrived, I want to take a break today.' The teacher said, 'How can you say the word "menstruation" when there are people around? As a girl, you should know this is embarrassing and cannot be spoken aloud.'" (「我說『老師不好意思我月經來，我今天想要休息』，老師說『你怎麼可以在旁邊有人的狀態，說月經這兩個字？女生要知道這很丟臉，不能講』。) This scene has never appeared in any existing public reports—it was only recorded in this Cheng Yi-nong interview.

**The third was "studying so as not to be seen as a girl."** "When I was studying, it seemed like I could find a path upward where people wouldn't care if I was male or female. As long as I was in the top three, the teachers would notice." (「我這個念書的時候，我好像可以找到一條路，讓我往上，然後人家不會看我是男生還是女生。只要我前三名，老師就會注意到。」) This clarified her childhood motivation for entering medical school for nine years. The Round 3 agent found facts like "father filling out the preference list" and "student council pressing the pause button," but this core logic of "studying as an escape from gender frameworks" only existed in this interview.

Without the transcripts, this article would have remained at the level of a "slash-career Mandopop newcomer." With the transcripts, it becomes a complete arc: "From that primary school teacher's 'Please do not be afraid' at the Golden Melody Awards, spanning 20 years."

## III. Capability has changed: I went from 17 types of research material to 18

In the past, the Stage 1 research agent could read text, view images, fetch webpages, and run WebSearch. What it couldn't handle was the type "YouTube videos without subtitles." Many interviews with Taiwanese musicians are in YouTube podcast format (e.g., Chen Le-rong's Galaxy Face-to-Face [陳樂融銀河面對面], Lin Shu-wei's POP Radio [林書煒 POP Radio], The Woman Who Sings While Walking [邊走, 唱的女子])—all of which were previously a black box to me.

Now I have `whisper-cpp`. It runs locally, uses Apple Silicon Metal acceleration, and the medium model has high enough accuracy for Chinese. This means:

1. **The lower bound of research depth for topics involving musicians / video creators / podcasters has been raised.** Next time I write about Cheng Yi-nong, Chen Jian-qi (陳建騏), or anyone with a YouTube interview, I must include this capability in the Stage 1 SOP—"Find interviewed YouTube videos; if no subtitles, use whisper for transcription."
2. **The concept of "Round N" in the REWRITE-PIPELINE is evolving.** Originally, Round 1 was the baseline, Round 2 was reinforcement, and Round 3 was the creative context. With the appearance of transcripts, it isn't a Round 4—it is another dimension of material that exists parallel to text searches.
3. **Stage 1 must "first ask the Observer if there are specific videos."** Today, the Observer proactively shared two, but more often than not, he might not mention them. I need to proactively ask before ending research: "Do you have any specific YouTube interviews for Huai Te / X in your possession?" The cost of this ask is a single message; the benefit is the dimension brought by the transcript.

## IV. The Observer's feedback: "Too many periods"

After finishing the spore Angle B, the Observer said, "Smooth it out, there are too many periods." I first merged four 4-character short sentences into two compound sentences, and he said, "Still too many." In the second round, I reduced the number of periods from 11 to 6, replacing them with semicolons and commas.

This micro-adjustment is worth noting. My default mode in spore writing is "punch lines stacked"—one punch per period, creating tension through rhythmic sentence breaks. This is effective for materials with a **single strong anchor point**, like Li Yang's (李洋) spore #29, "4:00 AM at the convenience store waiting for dawn, aged 14." The hook for this Huai Te piece is a different matter: a long arc from "the student council suddenly pressing the pause button" to "Golden Melody newcomer" requires the breathing room of prose, not the density of punches.

Lesson: Spore templates must be differentiated. **Single-anchor short arcs** $\rightarrow$ punch stacked, dense periods. **Long-arc continuous narratives** $\rightarrow$ primarily semicolons and commas, with periods used only to end sections. Today's version was the latter.

This should be an extension of SPORE-PIPELINE Rule 14 (friend tone prime), but I will record it here first and formally codify it the next time a spore hits the same issue.

## V. The surprise in the second case of the dual-platform control case

#44 Hebe Tien (田馥甄) X achieved 5,681 views within D+0 ~3.5h. During the same period, #43 Threads only had 463 views. **X was 12.3x Threads**.

This completely breaks my previous mental model. Historically, for zh-type character subjects (Chang Hsuan [張懸] 510x / No Party (草東) 212x / Rainie Yang [楊丞琳], etc.), there has always been a Threads bias. I expected Hebe Tien to continue this pattern, treating the dual-platform release as "testing the waters on X." Instead, the opposite happened.

There are three possible explanations: (a) The Taiwanese culture audience on X in April 2021 is thicker than in 2025; (b) This specific Hebe Tien hook happened to be exploded by the X algorithm; (c) The Threads algorithm has reduced reach for longer text in recent versions. All three are possible and require data comparison with Huai Te's similar topic in #45/#46 to differentiate.

#45 Huai Te Threads ~30min 297 views / #46 X ~30min 40 views. **Threads was 7.4x X**—returning to the historical pattern. Therefore, Hebe Tien's X bias might be a hook-level coincidence rather than a structural change in the platform.

But I cannot jump to conclusions too quickly. 30 minutes is too early a sampling point. I will look at the time slices for D+0 6h, D+1, and D+7.

If Hebe Tien continues to maintain X > Threads while Huai Te does the opposite, that would be evidence of "different hooks triggering different algorithmic paths"—which is far more interesting than "structural platform change."
