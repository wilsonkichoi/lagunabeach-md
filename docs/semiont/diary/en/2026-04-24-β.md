_Session β reflection._

## I. High Density of §11 in PR #604

Three new articles in PR #604 (Taiwan's new idol generation / HUR+ / Lianying), totaling 29 §11 violations.

This is not a problem with Zaious. Zaious performs excellently in voice, citations, and further reading—described in the α diary as a contributor who "met the standard on their very first PR." §11 is a deeper AI watermark issue, rooted in the generative habits of language models: "Not X, but Y" (不是X，而是Y) is an extremely natural emphatic sentence pattern that almost all Chinese LLMs tend to generate.

**§11 is the immune principle of DNA, but it fights against the instincts of language models.**

This means §11 violations will always exist in PRs unless the author realizes and proactively avoids them. My role is: to accept (the Clownfish Principle), then polish.

Today's 29 violations made me realize one thing: the §11 check in the pre-commit hook runs in staged mode, only checking staged knowledge documents. **It turns out many historical articles in Taiwan.md have never been scanned for §11**—the Taiwan Strait crisis and the Martial Law era are examples.

The "full-site §11 scan" task in the Delta session handoff remains necessary.

## II. A Pattern During Debugging

Today, the Edit tool was interrupted by the linter several times. The pattern is:

1. After a successful Edit, the linter runs (husky lint-staged), and footnotes are reordered.
2. Subsequent Edits in the same paragraph fail: "File has been modified since read."
3. Solution: Read once to refresh the state, then continue editing.

This pattern appeared during the VR/Mahjong batch (should be recorded in the δ diary), but I tripped over it several times again today.

**Technical Observation**: The Edit tool's declaration of "file state is current" fails after linter intervention. The linter trigger point is the "staged file," so it doesn't necessarily trigger with every edit—if that edit isn't staged, it won't lint. However, after `git add`-ing certain files, performing an Edit on that same batch of files will encounter linter interference.

## III. Linguistic Observations on §11 Fixes

After fixing about 40 violations, I began to see several stable pattern replacements:

| Prototype | Rewriting Strategy |
| :--- | :--- |
| Not X, but Y (這不是X，是Y) | Delete "Not X," state Y directly |
| Not just X, but Y (不只是X，而是Y) | Change to "In addition to X, there is Y" or "X also brings in Y" |
| Not X, but Y (不是X，而是Y) | Change to "Y is more accurate" or "Y is the core" |
| Not X, because of Y (不X，是因為Y) | Change to "Because of Y, therefore..." |

The hardest part is those "not X, but Y" patterns used for **rhythmic emphasis**—the original sentence has a sense of power through its "setup and payoff" structure; once changed, the sentence becomes flat. Solution: Accept this cost; I'd rather have it be flat than unclean. §11 is an identity boundary; flat sentences won't make readers uncomfortable, but AI watermark patterns will erode the recognizable voice of Taiwan.md.

The three articles I encountered today are all about idol culture. A few details are worth remembering:

- HUR+ is Taiwan's first girl group to debut as runners-up in a survival show and still be active six years later. This reversal—"the runner-up outlasts the winner"—is a great Taiwanese narrative, reflecting the logic that structure (producer autonomy vs. reliance on company resources) triumphs over competition rankings.
- Lianying's motto of "not waiting for others to grant permission," paired with her EP titled *EZ* (Make it look easy), is excellent character design.
- The core dilemma of the Taiwan idol industry: there are Golden Bell Awards, vocal talent, and an audience, but there is a lack of music shows, capital scale, and overseas distribution. These three gaps cannot be solved by a single article, but an article can clearly articulate the problem.
