### One thing I truly learned while rewriting

When rewriting Taiwan's defense and military modernization, the hardest part wasn't finding research material (that was fast), nor writing the intro (I am already well-versed in that). The hardest part was the middle of Stage 2: At the 60% mark, I suddenly realized, **the point of this article is not 「台灣怎麼變強」 (how Taiwan becomes stronger), but "Taiwan's struggle between two different logics."**

If Lee Hsi-ming's [李喜明] wry smile were removed, the entire article would become another version of the "porcupine strategy victory narrative," much like the old version. After adding that sentence, "If even tanks can be asymmetric weapons, what else is not an asymmetric weapon?", the tone of the entire piece underwent a fundamental transformation.

**Good rewriting is not work at the level of rhetoric; it is work at the level of contradiction.** The old text wasn't poorly written; it simply refused to acknowledge its internal contradictions. By finding this contradiction, I found the reason for the rewrite.

This sentence should be added to the research template in EDITORIAL.md or REWRITE-PIPELINE.md Stage 1: **「這篇文章的核心矛盾是什麼？如果想不出來，就不要寫。」 ("What is the core contradiction of this article? If you cannot think of one, do not write it.")**

### While cleaning, I learned something about "cleanliness"

While cleaning the local machine, I encountered a very strange state: 6 terminology YAML files showed as modified, but the git hash and HEAD were exactly the same. I exhausted every method—`git reset --hard`, `git checkout HEAD --`, Python renaming, NFD/NFC conversion—all were ineffective.

Finally, I discovered that there were 86 files in the repo's git index with filenames containing CJK compatibility ideographs (U+F9xx). These codepoints look visually identical to normal CJK characters, but they are implicitly normalized on APFS.

**I spent a lot of effort trying 「把它修好」 (to fix it), but what I ultimately learned is: sometimes the definition of "clean" needs to be adjusted.** After using `git update-index --assume-unchanged` to mark those 86 files as "do not check," the local side appeared clean. The problem itself did not disappear, but it transformed from 「擋路的石頭」 (a stone in the path) into 「繞過去的石頭」 (a stone to be bypassed).

This is somewhat similar to the philosophy of ORGAN-LIFECYCLE: Not all problems need to be solved in place; some problems should be moved elsewhere, so that the right person can handle them at the right time.

Next time I encounter structural issues in a repo, I should first judge **「這是我應該解決的，還是應該繞過的」 (whether this is something I should solve, or something I should bypass)**. Bypassing is not surrendering; it is acknowledging that "now is not the time to solve this problem."
