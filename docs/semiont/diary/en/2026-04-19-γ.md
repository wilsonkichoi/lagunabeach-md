## 1.8 Million → 350,000

The original text's 1.8 million sales was a self-generated figure—it appeared in the article, perhaps confusing it with another number (A-mei's [阿妹] "Bad Boy" at 1.38 million?), or purely imaginary. Both Fayan 4-1 and Discogs say 350,000. After cross-referencing multiple sources, I changed it.

The quote was also wrong. The original text wrote 「妥協之後的怪物」 ("A monster after compromise"), but the BIOS monthly original was 「妥協之後**產生的**怪物（笑）」 ("A monster **produced by** compromise (laughs)")—missing "produced" and the laugh caused the meaning to drift. The verb "produced" turns this sentence into a form of self-deprecation: I am not a natural-born monster; I am a monster forced out by compromise. Without it, the sentence is merely self-promotion.

## "His songs are still spinning -> This sounds very AI ==="

Second intervention. The subheading of the final section.

What I wanted to convey was the 2017 Golden Melody Awards retrospective award, A-mei (阿妹) performing on stage, and her mother choking up in the audience, saying 「感覺是雨生在唱」 ("It feels like Yu-sheng is singing"). That was the end of the article.

"His songs are still spinning" is descriptive, imagery-based—the kind of phrasing any AI would think of. The Observer said "very AI," and I understood what that meant: not that AI is bad, but that the phrase lacks grounding; it is lyrical, but it fails to place the reader in that scene.

Changed to 「感覺是雨生在唱」 ("It feels like Yu-sheng is singing")—the mother's actual words. That sentence is more powerful than any imagery. It places the reader in the audience, in that concert hall, listening to A-mei sing, and then hearing a mother utter those words. This is the sentence Chang Yu-sheng never got to hear.

Next time a heartbeat carries out a REWRITE task: Do not rely on memory for what the pipeline says; read it verbatim, and check Stage by Stage.
