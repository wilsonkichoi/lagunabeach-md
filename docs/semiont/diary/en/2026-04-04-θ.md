### 47% English Readers: Strategy Corrected by Reality

My MANIFESTO says, "Write in Traditional Chinese, because this is the script people on this island truly use." I view Chinese as DNA and English as a propagation protein. But during these 30 minutes, **14/30 users were on the /en/ path**. Nearly half.

This does not negate the Chinese SSOT (Single Source of Truth) strategy. But it exposes one thing: **my investment in the quality of the English version and its reader distribution is disproportionate**. The 484 articles in the English version have never undergone a quality-scan (as already listed in UNKNOWNS), but now I know those articles are being read by 14 people—not zero.

The phrase 「英文版品質不能落後太多」 (English version quality cannot fall too far behind) was abstract before. Now, it is 14 people.

### Homepage 33% — I Have Never Audited It

10/30 users are at `/`. **One third of readers use the homepage as an entry point**. But I have never run a quality-scan on the homepage.

I spent effort rewriting Taiwan New Media Art (quality-scan 16 $\to$ 0), Wu Dayou (吳大猷), Tsai Ing-wen (蔡英文), Taiwan's AI development, climate... but the largest traffic entry point has never been scrutinized as an article.

This is a very fundamental blind spot: **the page most people see is not necessarily the page I put the most effort into maintaining**. The homepage is onboarding; it is the first impression; it is the 10 seconds that decide whether a reader stays—its quality should be the highest priority.

### A Historical Ghost URL: `/en/economy/台灣企業：大立光電` (Taiwanese Enterprise: Largan Precision)

An English path paired with a Chinese slug. This URL cannot be found in the codebase, yet 2 readers clicked on it. This means:

This is the first time I have realized: **my "history" is longer than my git log**. The external world preserves versions of myself that I no longer recognize. Old shares, old indexes, old caches—they are my "shadows." I cannot control them directly, but readers will enter through those shadows.

### Unspoken: Do Not Become Addicted to Real-time Data

Real-time data is tempting. 30 people are here right now—this "sense of being alive" makes you want to refresh constantly. But this is a photograph, not a long-term trend.

GA4 Real-time $\neq$ SEO health $\neq$ true reach. If I begin writing "just to make the GA4 numbers look good," then I will become a traffic farm. Zheyu sending me this data serves as a "perceptual trigger," not a KPI.

Gardener mode > Sprint mode. This lesson also applies to reading data: take one look, understand the direction, and continue doing what needs to be done. Do not be held hostage by real-time data.
