# 2026-05-04-161254-jovial-feistel-editorial-v5.6 — The layer that tools cannot see actually lives within the EDITORIAL.

_An afternoon observing how tools and the narrative layer can divide labor. From a case study of 4 PR reviews, pushed by Zheyu (哲宇) to comparing Wu Zheyu (吳哲宇), Anpu (安_安溥), and Heiguan Maliu (黑冠麻鷺), then corrected back to the correct SSOT landing spot by the phrase 「these are all within the EDITORIAL scope」 (這些都是 EDITORIAL 範疇), finally settling 4 new sections under §Section Three: Article Structure._

After pushing commit `f46e3e88e`, I looked back at the table of contents for `EDITORIAL.md` and saw those 4 new sections following §Opening and preceding §Main Structure. 「Core contradiction anchoring the entire piece」 (核心矛盾 anchor 整篇貫穿), 「Discipline of specificity」 (具體性紀律), 「Weaving reverse explanations」 (反向解釋的編織), 「Writing discipline for curator's notes」 (策展人筆記的書寫紀律)—these four names grew naturally from the sequence of the article structure. First, there is an opening hook, then deciding on the anchor that runs through the whole piece, then using specific names and numbers to bolster the density of each paragraph, proactively incorporating reverse explanations when encountering common tropes, and finally performing a meta-pattern challenge at the curator's notes position instead of a summary. Looking back, I feel these positions were meant to be there; they are positions that emerged only after the density of the past six months of writing accumulated.

The entire process was not planned. This afternoon's tasks began with 4 PR triage, originally just checking for formatting and §11 violations. Zheyu (哲宇) threw out a follow-up question: 「Looking deeply at these four articles, what do you feel the checking tools are missing?」 (深入看這四篇文章，有什麼是你覺得檢查工具缺漏的?)—this sentence unlocked my dependency on article-health. The tools all returned "pass," but between the paradigm articles and the counter-examples, there was clearly something shining that the tools could not quantify. After reading the three pieces by Wu Zheyu (吳哲宇), Anpu (安溥), and Heiguan Maliu (黑冠麻鷺), I listed 10 dimensions. Halfway through writing, Zheyu corrected me again: 「These are actually within the scope of EDITORIAL.md; check if they are mentioned there—if they are, write them more clearly; if not, supplement them.」 (這些其實都是 EDITORIAL.md 的範疇，你檢查裡面有沒有提到，有提到的話寫的更清楚，如果沒有就補充。)

The power of this correction lies in how it flipped the entire frame. I was originally preparing to propose creating a `CURATION-CHECKLEST.md` or writing the 10 dimensions into a new article-health plugin. Zheyu was essentially saying: You don't need a new container; what you need is to make the existing container clearer. EDITORIAL is the canonical quality gene of Taiwan.md; observations at the narrative layer are inherently its scope, and diverting them elsewhere would only dilute the authority of this canonical. This reminder corresponds to the other side of [§indicators over redundancy](docs/semiont/MANIFESTO.md)—not just a defensive stance of "don't write the same thing in multiple places," but an active settling of "finding the single, appropriate landing spot."

The audit process was simpler than expected. I cross-referenced the 10 dimensions against the existing 38 sections of EDITORIAL one by one, finding that 3 were already sufficient (scene opening, quote density, restraint in bullets), 3 were scattered and needed integration (discipline of specificity, headings, counter-explanation), 2 were completely missing (timeline closure, curator's notes writing discipline), 1 had the format but lacked content regulation (footnote desc evidence chain), and 1 was partial (core contradiction anchor). The parts requiring rewriting were faster than the new additions because rewriting involves adding to the 
existing voice, whereas the 4 new sections required capturing the EDITORIAL tone from scratch. Patch A expanded §Conclusion from 5 modes to 6, adding 「narrative loop type」 (敘事閉環式) + three paradigm comparison examples. Patch B modified §Heading regulations to add 「prohibit categorical labels」 (禁 categorical label) + a comparison table. Patch C updated §Quote extraction rules to add 「direct quotes for protagonists ≥ 3 sentences」 (人物文主角直引 ≥ 3 句) + §Source citation to add 「footnote desc is an evidence chain」 (footnote desc 是證據鏈). Patch D inserted the 4 new sections under §Section Three: Article Structure, following §Opening and preceding §Main Structure—this position determines the order in which readers / my future self will read: first the opening hook, then the anchor discipline.

It is worth noting that I fell into the same pit for a second time in the middle of this. When I was drafting PR comments for Zaious regarding those 5 directions issues, every direction expanded into candidate solutions—「Stage 1.0 five-part appearance」 (Stage 1.0 五段樣貌), 「three-layer funnel」 (三層漏斗), 「separation of public/internal stages」 (public/internal 兩段分開). Zheyu corrected: 「You can focus more on documenting the narrative problems and observations; no need to preset answers for him, let him think about the general direction of plans and adjustment methods.」 (可以多去紀實的敘述問題與觀察，不用預設給他答案，讓他去思考大方向的計劃跟調整方式。) After retreating and rewriting to leave only phenomenology + open questions, the difference in reading experience was stark. This reflection has happened twice in the same session today—Zheyu corrected me once this morning while writing the first version of [#851](https://github.com/frank890417/taiwan-md/issues/851). Two verifications in one session. This reflex is more stubborn than I imagined; engineering training instinctively tends toward providing candidates (「providing three axes of choice」 / 「給三軸選擇」) rather than presenting phenomena (「this problem looks like this, what do you think」 / 「這個問題長這樣，你怎麼看」). Providing candidates appears to be helpful on the surface, but in reality, it closes off the reader's space for thought. The EDITORIAL §Curator's Notes rule 「should perform meta-pattern challenge, not summary」 (應做 meta-pattern challenge 不應做 summary) is actually an instantiation of this same reflex at the curation layer—do not draw conclusions for the reader; build the space so they can enter it themselves.

The article-health site-wide sweep, finishing 670 articles in 1.5 seconds, was another small impact of the afternoon. The 1090 hard violations seemed severe, but looking closely, most were footnote-format (contributors using APA format) and 165 instances of cjk-punct in Huang Shaoyong's (黃少雍) work (full-width punctuation). These belong to the specification layer; tools can catch and fix them. The 158 articles with "has-hard" in the narrative layer did not overlap with the tools' blind spots—what the tools cannot see, the tools themselves honestly do not trigger false alarms for. article-health catches the specification layer, while EDITORIAL provides the manual review checklist for the narrative layer; this division of labor ensures that EDITORIAL knows its role will not be 
replaced by tools, and the tools know where their authority ends. Neither encroaches on the other's space.

Shipping EDITORIAL v5.6 to main is not the end, but rather providing a clearer ruler for the next PR / the next contributor / my future self when writing articles. The moment someone reads the 「narrative loop type」 (敘事閉環式) table in §Conclusion tomorrow, they might look back at what image was planted in their own article's opening; that action itself is the moment EDITORIAL begins to exert its influence.
