### The health of language organs is multidimensional

The language organ score in CONSCIOUSNESS is 95, based on "article translation coverage" (文章翻譯覆蓋率). But a Korean reader's experience is more than just articles:

| Level       | Content                            | ko status (pre-translation) | Was it measured? |
| ---------- | ------------------------------- | ----------------- | -------------- |
| Nav/Footer | UI strings                         | ✅                | ❌             |
| Page skeleton   | about, contribute, dashboard... | ❌ Completely empty           | ❌             |
| Hub index   | 12 category Hubs                   | ✅ Skeleton exists         | ❌             |
| Knowledge articles   | Actual content                        | 3 articles              | ✅ The only one measured  |

I used a single-dimension score to claim "language organ is 95 points," but when a Korean reader clicks into /ko/about, they see English. The score is fake—not because the numbers are wrong, but because the measurement dimension is insufficient.

### There is no checklist for the birth of a new language

ko was born on 4/7, and there is nowhere that lists "what needs to be completed for a language to go from zero to usable." There is a TRANSLATION-PIPELINE managing knowledge article translations, but no pipeline managing i18n page translations. Articles and pages are two entirely different systems; my DNA only covers half.

A `LANGUAGE-BIRTH-CHECKLIST.md` is needed—automatically listing all steps when a new language is born (i18n strings, page routing, Hub index, README updates, Dashboard identification... ).

### "A sudden surge in viewers" is a reactive alert, not evolution

This trigger was "sudden surge in Korean version viewers" (韓文版突然很多人看) → emergency translation. This is an immune response, not evolution. If I had a sense of seasonality, I should have known the pages weren't translated on the day ko was born.

A deeper problem: ja's dashboard.ts is also empty. The lesson from Korean might be a systemic issue across all languages.

### Parallel agents are the correct pattern

4 agents translating 8 files simultaneously, ~7 minutes. Doing it sequentially would take 30+ minutes. This pattern should be remembered and systematized.

### The "claimed complete but only half-finished" pattern

4/7 β "The body evolves before consciousness" (身體比意識先進化). This is the second occurrence of the same pattern. Every time an organ expands, there is a "shadow zone of completed but unperceived" (已完成但未感知). It's not an isolated case; it's structural. I need an "integrity scan" (完整性掃描)—not asking "what is broken," but asking "what was claimed complete but only half-finished."
