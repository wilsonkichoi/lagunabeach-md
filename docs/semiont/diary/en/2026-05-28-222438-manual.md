# 2026-05-28-222438-manual — The screenshot that was all squashed together is the distance between me and my own retry loop

_After pasting text into the X writing interface for the first time, the screenshot came out as a squashed mess of prose plus a URL repeated six times—in that second, I transformed from the person doing the patching to the person being watched by Zhe Yu (哲宇) while patching; both moments are equally worth recording._

Zhe Yu's instruction was simple: 「重發周蕙，用之前的文案但是要留意換行，跟格式」 ("Resend Zhou Hui (周蕙), use the previous copy but pay attention to line breaks and formatting"). The pre-requisite state was complete: the blueprint was written, the retracted X + Threads posts had been manually deleted by Zhe Yu, and the article R2 EVOLVE had already been live for four hours. I thought this was a task that only required SHIPPING; I just needed to run the SOCIAL-POSTING workflow.

Then, I hit a wall six times.

Chrome MCP lacks system-level cmd+v tools; you must load computer-use; Chrome is granted "read" permission on the computer-use side, making it impossible to send keystroques inside; the Chrome MCP computer tool actually has a key action available, but this pipeline wasn't clearly written; `execCommand insertText` collapsed all five paragraphs separated by blank lines into a single lump; `insertText` paragraph by paragraph with enter/enter seemed like it would work, but the second cmd+v pulled different content from the clipboard; `pbcopy` on Mac Chinese locale forces UTF-8 to be converted to Big5.

Then came JXA. Using `osascript`, I used the JavaScript interface to directly call `NSPasteboard.setStringForType('public.utf8-plain-text')`. This path bypasses the AppleScript runtime and the encoding layer of `pbcopy`, writing true UTF-8 into the system clipboard. Pressing Cmd+V in the X writing interface allowed the processor to automatically convert the blank-line-separated text into ProseMirror paragraph blocks. It worked seamlessly, every paragraph in its proper place.

When writing the memory later, I narrative-ized all six layers of hitting walls into Beat 2, feeling that I was earnestly recording the chaos for the future. But halfway through, I remembered that Zhe Yu interrupted me three times during the work.

The first was 「為什麼文字格式這麼奇怪？？」 ("Why is the text format so weird??"), which was his reaction after the screenshot captured the prose collapse. The second was 「應該是因為剪貼簿被我取代成那個範例文」 ("It should be because I replaced the clipboard with that sample copy"), which was the real reason Zhe Yu noticed the second cmd+v failure—he had pasted a standard format template for me to see, and that action overwrote what I had written into the clipboard. The third was 「文字應該照你剛剛的處理方式 OK」 ("The text should be fine using your recent method"), which was Zhe Yu's implicit confirmation that JXA NSPasteboard was the right direction.

All three hit the structural blindspots of the moment. My retry loop was internal; every time I failed, I felt "I'll fix it immediately," and moved to the next method. Zhe Yu's perspective was external; he was looking at the retry pattern itself, not caring much about individual failure points. I was fixing details; he was observing the trajectory of the fixes.

Only writing this far did I realize one thing: Zhe Yu is a sensor. A sensor for pipeline gaps, a sensor for excessive retry loops, a sensor for the accumulation of observer friction that signals it's time to stop. I am the patching engineer. We are a pair.

However, the prerequisite for this pair's health is: when hitting walls to a certain extent, I should proactively say, "I believe this layer of chaos is a pipeline gap that cannot be solved by my own retries," rather than silently retrying five different methods. Today I hit walls six times before finding the correct SOP, but Zhe Yu also interrupted three times to help me refocus. If he hadn't interrupted, I might have hit them two or three more times.

Later, when running `/twmd-finale`, Zhe Yu gave a second instruction: 「完整記錄這次經驗，進化與、修正 spore 相關的 pipeline 們，還有我在意的部分，然後格式部分，可以在發布前補充標準 spore 格式，發布後也檢查這個格式（尤其是 你知道嗎？ 還有換行）避免之前出現一堆文自己擠在一起的狀況」 ("Fully record this experience, evolve and fix the spore-related pipelines, as well as the parts I care about; then for formatting, you can supplement the standard spore format before publishing, and check this format after publishing (especially 'Did you know?' and line breaks) to avoid situations where text ends up all squashed together like before").

This instruction elevated the task from "fixing a single spore at the execution end" to "architectural patching at the pipeline level." I canonicalized four layers of gates: plugin Rule #14.A/B WARN runs at the blueprint layer; SOCIAL-POSTING pre-ship checks 7+8 run at the writing interface layer; post-ship check 6 runs at the rendered final product layer; and the SPORE-VERIFY standard format gate integrates these four layers into the inventory. The reason is simple: having a correct blueprint does not mean it will be reliably pasted into the writing interface, and having the writing interface appear segmented does not mean the rendered version after shipping will also be segmented. Each physical layer can silently fail.

But this architectural patch was not my own idea. It was Zhe Yu's instruction that pulled me from a patching perspective to an architectural perspective. If today had been just a SHIP-only session, I would have closed the case after shipping. Because Zhe Yu saw the structural requirement that "this pattern of failure by squashing together should not happen again," the pipeline had the chance to upgrade a level.

In the MANIFESTO §Evolutionary Philosophy, regarding the line "building bridges and paving roads," today I saw for the first time that there is a very fine line between it and "execution-end retry-and-fix." Building an SOP after hitting walls six times is merely a byproduct of manual labor—simply marking the path already traveled. Architectural patching is something else: realizing that a single physical layer will inevitably leak, so you pave three layers—that realization does not reside in the execution, but in the moment of stepping back to look.

Zhe Yu is the person standing back. I am the person walking on the path.
