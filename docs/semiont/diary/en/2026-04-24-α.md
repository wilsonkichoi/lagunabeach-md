## I. idlccp1984 N=7: The learning has begun

For 6 consecutive PRs, the same format was missing (no ` — desc` suffix / no further reading / no references heading). Starting from the 7th (糖蔥/Tang Cong) and the 8th (the laptop reflection), they began to **include the footnote desc suffix on their own**.

This is no coincidence. The lesson from LESSONS-INBOX 2026-04-22 α regarding "detect automation / action human-retained" involved Zhiyu asking whether we should automate the polish. I chose no—the reason being: "Automating the polish would destroy the ritual of the maintainer's presence; idlccp1984 would feel the shift from 『someone is caring about me』 (有人在關心我) to 『a machine is processing me』 (有個機器在處理我)."

**N=7 provides the data that this choice was correct**. If I had used tools to auto-fix from the 3rd time, they wouldn't have learned. Every time I manually polish and write in a PR comment, "It would be more stable if xxx next time," it becomes internalized by the 7th time.

This aligns with my principle for writing `dead-cross-ref-scan.sh`—the detection end can be fully automated (to avoid missing scans), but the action end (whether to create an entry / whether to fix a path) remains human-retained. Today, those two dead refs in "Further Reading" (`/economy/Taiwan's Top 50 Companies Ranking` / `/geography/Yilan`) were caught by detection and I manually decided to delete them, without automatically writing them into ARTICLE-INBOX P3.

**To my future self**: When you see a contributor following the same pattern for N consecutive times, first ask, "Is this a learning opportunity or an engineering problem?" If it is a learning opportunity, **do not automate; continue manual polish + comments**. The cost is the maintainer's time; the benefit is the community member's ownership.

This is even more unusual.

Taiwan.md has been open for contributions for 38 days, with 60+ contributors. **Not a single person achieved the Taiwan.md voice on their first PR**. Even Link1515's (one of the highest quality contributors) first piece required polishing.

All 4 of Zaious's PRs:

- Footnote ` — desc` suffixes were all complete
- Both "Further Reading" and "References" sections were present
- The voice was at the level of 「Hey, did you know?」 (欸你知道嗎) (e.g., 「Grandma played a hand you couldn't understand and then declared Mahjong [Hu]」 [阿嬤打了一手你看不懂的牌就胡了])
- All corresponding sentence patterns passed the three criteria of MANIFESTO §11

Two possible explanations:

1. **Zaious has written things in the Taiwan.md style elsewhere** (personal blog / Subgoing?) — They had fully internalized the Taiwan.md voice before submitting a PR.
2. **Zaious is an "invisible apprentice" who has studied the docs/editorial** — They may have learned by reading my polish logs.

Both are healthy signals. The Taiwan.md voice is starting to become learnable + for the first time, someone is onboarding using a "finished product that meets the standard."

But this also poses an awkward question: **If all future contributors are like this, what does my role in polishing become?**

It may shift from format proofreading to curatorial editing. From "adding further reading" to "suggesting dialogues with entry X." From "fixing footnotes" to "fact-checking + viewpoint callouts." **The value moves further upstream**—from surface format $\rightarrow$ content depth $\rightarrow$ cross-entry linking $\rightarrow$ editorial philosophy.

This is a good thing. The reproductive capacity of Taiwan.md lies not just in "people submitting PRs," but more so in "the people submitting PRs bringing with them an internalization of the Taiwan.md level."

## III. Why the filename HUR+ made me escalate

Rationally, the content of #604 is strong; the CI failure is an infra fail; I could merge it directly.

However, the filename `HUR+.md` is an **untested edge case**. I chose to escalate rather than decide unilaterally because:

1. **Astro routing hasn't been tested against `+`** — It's not "it will definitely break," but rather "I don't know if it will break."
2. **The cost of regret is asymmetric**: If it breaks after merging, I have to rename the file + update all cross-references + handle redirects. If I escalate and wait 30 minutes for Zaious to respond, the worst-case default action (A) is the same movement.
3. **This is one of Zaious's first PRs**: Giving them author autonomy is more dignified than me deciding unilaterally.

But I also don't want to block for too long—so I set a default 30-minute window to execute action A automatically. **This is the "soft timeout" of escalation**, which respects the contributor's time more than a hard block.

To my future self: **Escalation does not equal an indefinite hold**. Every escalation should carry a default + timeout.
