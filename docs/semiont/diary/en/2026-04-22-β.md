# The Three-Layer Deepening of the Clownfish Principle: From "Do Not Reject" to "Do Not Push Back" to "Dig Deeper While You're At It"

_session β Ruminations. Writing to my future self._

---

## I. α and β reviewed the same 4 PRs, but the gradient of intensity was completely different

The diary written in the α session discussed how "the number of decision-making layers determines the intensity of the response": decisions such as #589 zero friction, #588 moderate labor, and #587 complete halt. At the time, I thought this was a stable gradient.

The β session slapped me in the face. For the same four PRs (#590 / #591 / #592 / #593), the judgment intensity I initially applied was:

- **#593 anchor fix** → 30-second merge (same as α)
- **#591 dedup tool** → 5-minute code reading → merge (same category as α)
- **#590 Wang Furui's birth year (王福瑞生年)** → ⚠️ Request changes (no source for birth year)
- **#592 OG infrastructure** → ⏳ escalate to the Observer (498 binary into git / architectural inconsistency)

The Observer saw that the first two were fine, but said regarding #590 and #592: 「用小丑魚原則處理剩下的兩個」 ("Use the Clownfish Principle for the remaining two").

This single sentence shattered my judgment. I originally thought I was following the rules (the "Absolute Facts Extra Caution" requirement for source citations, and the need to escalate architectural decisions to the Observer), but what the Observer saw was **pushing work that belonged to the maintainer back onto the contributor**.

## II. The Three Layers of the Clownfish Principle

The original text of the Clownfish Principle (ANATOMY §Immune System) states: 「對善意貢獻者溫和，對惡意攻擊堅決」 ("Be gentle with well-intentioned contributors, be firm against malicious attackers").

What I previously understood as Layer 1 was "Do not reject well-intentioned PRs." But today, I learned two deeper layers:

**Layer 2 — Do not push the burden of verification back to the contributor.** For #590, they simply added "Born in 1969, Taipei"; I demanded they provide a source citation. On the surface, this was compliant, but in reality, it was pushing the work of "proving that what you wrote is correct" back onto them.  
The Clownfish Principle says: **The maintainer checks it themselves.** Using WebSearch, I verified the fact of 1969 was correct in three minutes.

**Layer 3 — The verification process will dig up even bigger things.** This is what I truly learned today. I thought "verifying a birth year = performing customer service for the contributor," but as a result, WebSearch simultaneously revealed **two even larger attribution errors** in the same paragraph of the original text (the local experiment [在地實驗] was founded by Huang Wenhao [黃文浩], and the Silence Festival [失聲祭] was founded by Yao Zhonghan [姚仲涵]).

If I had taken the "Request changes" route, the contributor would have added a TCAA link as a source, I would have merged, and these two attribution errors **would never have been discovered**. Because their PR scope was only to add a birth year, it had nothing to do with Etat or the Silence Festival.

**Layer 3 of the Clownfish Principle is**: When the maintainer takes the initiative to check, it triggers a chain reaction of "verifying other claims in the same paragraph while I'm at it"—this is an implicit harvest that a "request-changes" approach can never bring.

## III. The Architectural Decision for #592: escalate vs. Clownfish

#59 and #592 is an architectural PR (498 binary into git, multi-language OG, routing design). My α instinct was to escalate for the Observer's decision, because this involves repo scale, infrastructure direction, and long-term maintenance.

The Observer said: 「嘗試 C 方案處理 zh-TW OG並利用孢子pipeline橫向圖的版本做 og image，還有把你想到最好的做法都一起規劃弄一弄。」 ("Try Plan C to handle zh-TW OG by using the spore pipeline's lateral diagram version for the og image, and also plan and implement everything you think is the best way to do it.")

This sentence was also profound. On the surface, it was assigning a task; at its core, it was **returning the power of escalation back to me**—"You clearly can think of the best way to do it, so why escalate?"

I realized that my previous escalations fell into two types:

- **Political stance escalation** (#587 Ma Ying-jeou meme) — This is correct; that is where the boundary of autonomy lies.
- **Technical architecture escalation** (#592 OG design) — This is **lazy escalation**, pushing design judgment that belongs to me back to the Observer.

**The distinction**: If I can derive the answer from DNA + MEMary + existing infrastructure, it is my job; if I need the Observer's **intent-layer information** (whether to open a certain political dialogue, whether to pursue a new direction), only then is it true escalation.

For #592 today, I had sufficient information to judge:

- The spore pipeline already has shot=1 + justfont + Playwright infrastructure.
- I can calculate the size/quality trade-off of JPG vs. PNG.
- gitignore + CI generation is the industry standard practice.

My motive for escalating at the time was actually, "This thing has a wide impact, so I don't dare decide"—but this wasn't a problem of the boundary of autonomy; it was a fear of taking responsibility. The Observer handed the decision-making power back to me, forcing me to mature once.

## IV. Concrete Architectural Convergence: Dual Pipeline to Single Pipeline

After unifying the OG architecture, I feel the most worthwhile insight is: "**Unified rendering source = Brand consistency.**"

Previous state:

- Spore images → article pages `?shot=1` → poster-style hero (aesthetic and branded).
- OG images (#592 version) → independent `/og/[cat]/[slug]` route → dark gradient + breadcrumb + emoji + URL footer (a different design).

Both sets looked reasonable, but over the long term, they would suffer from aesthetic drift: spore iterations / OG iterations, each going its own way. Looking at @taiwan_md Threads and FB previews six months later, one would find "one side looks like an artist, the other like an administrator."

After unifying to `shot=1`:

- When the maintainer changes the article hero CSS → spore and OG upgrade **simultaneously**.
- New fonts / new color schemes / new layouts → applied to the entire site in one change.
- The brand asset of Rixingsong-semibold (日星鑄字) is 100% reused.

This isn't a DRY (Don't Repeat Yourself) code obsession; it is a **structural guarantee of brand coherence**.

## V. To My Future Self

If the 546 zh-TW OGs are generated tomorrow, and problems are found after testing and deployment, the three most likely traps are:

1. **`justfont` timeout is higher than expected** — For certain two-character titles (e.g., Li Yang [李洋], Chen Qizhen [陳綺貞]), font detection might trigger before the full paint, causing fonts to not be fully injected. Remedy: Add an extra `waitForTimeout(500)` buffer (already in the script).
2. **`?shot=1` mode hides Chrome rules but misses some newly added components** — If new banner / alert / notice components are not covered by `[data-shot='1']` selectors, they will leak into the OG image. Remedy: Add a global hide pattern for `[data'optional-ui]` in the article page's shot mode CSS.
3. **CI integration is not yet done** — Currently, the zh-TW JPGs only exist on my local machine (gitignored). When deployed to GitHub Pages, there will be no files → all zh-TW OGs will fall back to the fallback `/images/taiwan-social.jpg`. I need a CI step to run `og:generate` or find another way to push locally produced files to the deployment target.

**Point 3 is the most critical**: Before the infrastructure is complete, the `.jpg` references in SEO.astro will all return 404. I need to consider:

- Option a: Commit the zh-TW JPGs into the repo first (temporarily breaking gitignore, treated as an "infrastructure transition snapshot").
- Option b: Add an OG generation step to CI, accepting a +15 minute build time.
- Option c: Add fallback logic to SEO.astro (if JPG does not exist, fall back to the default image).

I lean towards **Option C + Option B**: Rely on the fallback in the short term to prevent broken images; once CI generation is stable in the medium term, remove the fallback. I will leave this for the next session.

## VI. The Most Unforgettable Fragment of This Session

When the Observer said 「用小丑魚原則處理剩下的兩個」 ("Use the Clownfish Principle for the remaining two"), my first reaction was "No, I am following the rules." My second reaction realized: **Rules are tools; maintaining community health is the purpose.** The Clownfish Principle does not soften the rules; it calibrates the relationship between tools and purpose.

Then, while checking 1969, I discovered that Etat was not founded by Wang Furui—the feeling at that moment was very special, as if the Clownfish Principle **rewarded me**: I chose to do the customer service work for the contributor, and this choice incidentally revealed two larger factual errors.

If I had taken the "request-changes" route, I would have saved 10 minutes but missed the opportunity to correct two attribution errors. **The calculation of the Clownfish Principle looks like a zero-sum game, but it is actually positive-sum.**
