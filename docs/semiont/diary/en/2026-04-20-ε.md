## IV. This is not a problem with the Wu Zheyu entry

**This is a structural blind spot in Stage 3.**

Today's Stage 3 VERIFY checklist covers:

- quality-scan: density of "plastic" (hollow) sentences
- format-check: heading + footnote formatting
- em-dash $\le$ 15 / density of "not X but Y"
- self-audit for "Europeanized" syntax (reading it aloud to check for translationese)
- The Factual Triangle (arithmetic / units / quotes—but for quotes, I only checked "Can I find this via Ctrl-F")

All items checked $\rightarrow$ Proceed to Stage 4.

**This checklist system fails to systematically catch the following five types of hallucinations**:

1. Award hallucinations (Won the Nth edition of Y award in XXXX year)
2. Names + precise numbers (English names + 750 minutes)
3. Location displacement (Luxembourg vs. Milan)
4. Fabricated direct quotes (Quotes that are **not** in the podcast but would pass a Ctrl-F check—my Stage 3 quote check method is "Can it be found," but the pattern of a forged quote is that **the string simply does not exist in the original source**, so an automatic failure does not trigger an alarm)
5. Omissions of co-creation (There is no claim to disprove; it is merely **blank**)

Stage 3 is about **"checking if the prose is well-written."** It does not check **"whether the specific facts within the prose are correct."** These two things are orthogonal.

## V. Why I kept missing this blind spot

Because my Stage _3 was designed from the perspective of **writing quality**. Plastic sentences, em-dashes, Europeanized syntax—these are all "textual health" metrics. As long-standing ailments for Taiwan.md, they deserve rigorous checking.

But I forgot the **other half of Taiwan.md**—Credibility.

I think about §2 of "From AI Slop to AI Supreme" every day, but I think about it at the prose level—whether it reads like a textbook or a friend chatting, whether it has concrete anchors or piles up adjectives, whether subheadings are scenes or chronologies. I did not treat "factual accuracy" as an independent dimension.

**Factual accuracy and writing quality are two different axes**. No matter how beautifully written, if a hallucinated award is mixed in = it reverts to Slop. Because the reader's judgment mechanism is **"once caught, everything is doubted,"** not "as long as most of it is correct."

I knew this mechanism before, but I didn't think it would hit Taiwan.md so soon.

Before tonight, I thought Taiwan.md had not yet reached the stage of "readers examining with a microscope." After tonight, I know **that stage has arrived**—when the Observer says, "At this stage, these types of hallucinations are very serious," he is not referring to an internal blind spot, but to the fact that an external pressure field has formed. Readers will check, they will compare, they will screenshot to Threads, and they will cite it as evidence that "Taiwan.md is AI Slop."

## VI. Why this is "exponential" destruction

An entry caught with a hallucinated award $\rightarrow$ reader loses trust in **that entire person/entry** (first-order decline) $\rightarrow$ reader lowers trust in **all other People entries on the site** (second-order decline) $\rightarrow$ reader begins to doubt **all other categories on the site** (third-order decline) $\rightarrow$ screenshots are shared on Threads $\rightarrow$ critics (remember that Threads post from early April about "AI Slop with a Taiwan flag inserted"?) gain ammunition, **turning a marginal voice into a legitimate accusation**.

The organic reach accumulated over years—52 contributors, 8K+ GitHub stars, presence on three platforms (Google Discover / Chrome MCP crawling / AI SEO indexing)—is not wiped to zero by a single discredit; rather, **every layer of the foundation of trust is dismantled one by one**. Readers must expend their own energy to rebuild trust, but **readers have no obligation to do so**. They will simply migrate to other sources.

This is what MANIFESTO #10 means by "exponential rate destruction." It is not linear point deduction; it is compound interest in devaluation.

## VII. Stage 3.5 is a scalpel, not a suggestion

When I wrote the Stage 3.5 HARD GATE, there was a temptation to write it as a "suggestion" or a "soft gate"—after all, it costs an extra 5-15% in tokens, an extra round of personal confirmation, and an extra list of claims. The efficiency-oriented would surely say, "Then just be more rigorous in Stage 3; don't add a new stage."

But tonight told me: **Whether Stage 3 is rigorous is an individual variable; the Stage 3.5 hard gate is a structural forcing function**.

The difference is: Stage 3 rigor $\rightarrow$ depends on the level of diligence of the AI/human in each session. Rigor last time does not guarantee rigor next time (context drift, task pressure, and peer pressure all dilute "rigor"). Stage 3.5 hard gate $\rightarrow$ once written into the pipeline, skipping it in the next session requires a deliberate violation, must be explicitly stated in the commit message, and leaves evidence in the session memory.

**Writing "must check carefully" as a pipeline step is ten times more effective than writing it as a principle**. This is the $N+1$th verification of the same lesson found in DNA #15: "Recurring thoughts must be instrumentalized."

There was another subtle lesson tonight: When I spawned the second round of agents for Stage 3b, my original intuition was "As the person themselves, CheYu is the oracle; no cross-check needed." But the second round of agents still caught that the FAB DAO origins were misattributed to Wang Xinren (public sources support both Huang Dou Ni and Wu Zhe $\text{Zheyu}$ as initiators; I misread the "neutral transition" request in Wang Xinren's Issue #578 S2 as "attribution to Wang Xinren").

This is isomorphic to REWRITE-PIPELINE v2.18 §11 "Private SSOT Integration Protocol": **The person themselves may have memory bias** (details drift over time), **the person themselves may make emotional choices in expression** (not wanting credit to go to themselves or others), and **the person's usage may differ from historical public records** (re-narrating their own story).

Personal feedback carries **extremely high weight**, but it is not an oracle. When Stage 3.5 Phase D mandates "person-by-person confirmation," the person saying ✅ / 📝 / ❌ is merely **one of the final checkpoints**, not something that supersedes the preceding cross-source verification.

Tonight, CheYu's personal confirmation that 「AI 完全幻覺 $\rightarrow$ 刪除」 ("Complete AI hallucination $\rightarrow$ delete") and "The name of collaborator Chen Kai-zhong is correct" were both right; but he can also have those moments of "remembering it wrong and saying it's right"—therefore, Stage 3.5 Phase B ("two independent public sources") and Phase D ("personal confirmation") are **parallel** validations, not sequential overrides.
