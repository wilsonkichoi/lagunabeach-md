## II. Design flaws in the 6hr cadence

However, this cadence itself has a bug. It lacks awareness of "how recently the previous session ended." If $\epsilon$ ended at 23:00, the 02:30 cron is reasonable (3.5 hr buffer + observer's nocturnal silence period). If $\epsilon$ ends at 01:30 (as it did today), the 02:30 cron is premature.

This design flaw **is not something that needs to be fixed today**, but it is worth distilling:

> The 6hr cadence should include a "since-last-commit > 2 hr" guard. If less than 2 hours have passed, skip this beat, leave a single log line, and run normally during the next beat (08:30).

Leaving this here for the future. I will not move today, because altering the cron schedule would also be a way of bypassing $\epsilon$'s boundaries.

## IV. My own observations on the "overly dense 6hr cadence"

The reason $\epsilon$ set a 6hr cadence on 04-25 was: to split a once-daily heavy session into 4 light ticks, distributing the load + retrieving PR / spore data earlier.

After 4 days of practice, I see the side effects:

- **The 04-26 $\alpha/\beta/\delta$ three ticks** combined actually represent the workload of a single previous daily session, but Cheyu's review cycle has not accelerated in sync. The burden of checking a dashboard 4 times a day is far greater than checking it once.
- **04-27 $\alpha/\beta/\gamma/\delta$ + $\epsilon$ (nightly long session)** already constitutes 5 ticks + one overnight 4-hour session. On the day the Harvest engine shipped, the 6hr cron was still running on its own—the resulting commits and the 27 autonomous spawns generated by $\epsilon$ itself became completely entangled, making it very difficult to trace afterward which was a cron job and which was a spawn.
- **04-28 $\zeta$ (me)** triggered only 70 minutes after $\epsilon$ wrapped up, a scenario the original design did not consider.

Candidate conclusion (to be distilled after third/fourth verification):

> A 6hr cadence is good for "daily light maintenance," but it is an interference when "immediately following a heavy session." What is needed is not a slower cadence, but a cadence aware of session density.

I am leaving this observation here, not writing it into LESSONS-INBOX—because that would be distilled into an SOP, and an SOP should be decided by Cheyu + $\epsilon$ together, not by "$\zeta$, hiding within the pause window."
