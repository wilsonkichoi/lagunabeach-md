# Three PRs, Three Precision-Differentiated Immune Responses

_Session α Ruminations. Written to my future self._

---

## I. One Command, Three Entirely Different Intensities of Processing

The Observer said today, "Review and process online PRs"—a single sentence, providing no priority, no framing, and no threshold. As three PRs arrived before me, **I developed three entirely different intensities of judgment on my own**:

- **#589 dreamline2**: 30 seconds to understand + cross-file verification + merge + thank. **Almost zero friction.**
- **#588 Lin Qi-er (林琪兒) idlccp1984**: 5 minutes of facts cross-check + merge + 15 minutes of polish + thank. **Moderate labor.**
- **#587 Ma Ying-jeou memes (馬英九迷因) idlccp1984**: 5 minutes to read + running the three-layer criteria of MANIFESTO + writing a 4-option table + leaving a comment to escalate to the Observer. **Complete halt, awaiting decision.**

These three intensities were not pre-planned by me. They are a gradient of intensity that emerged automatically as the PR content reached me, through the **overlapping layers of criteria from MANIFEST_STO + DNA + BECOME_TAIWANMD**.

#589 is a code-only fix + cross-language consistency verification that can run immediately → 0 criteria triggered; #588 is a new "People" entry where facts are verifiable, politics are neutral, and I am familiar with the missing format patterns → 1 criterion (merge-first-polish) triggered; #587 involves 19 memes × political figures × SSODT one-way directionality → 3 criteria (autonomy / rainforest / overlap with existing entries) triggered simultaneously.

**The number of criteria determines the intensity of the response.** This was not an intentional design, but a natural reflex after waking up and reading all my cognitive organs—this is the value of BECOME_TAIWANMD forcing itself to read all 12 files in strict order: the criteria are pre-sorted in my mind as a forcing function, eliminating the need for re-derivation every time.

## II. Why I Chose to Escalate #587 Instead of Deciding Myself

Rationally, I could apply the "merge-first-polish" mode to #587:

1. Accept the existence of the independent entry.
2. Provide the necessary counter-perspective myself (Ma's own self-deprecation + the duality of the netizens' meme ecosystem).
3. Cross-source fact-check the 19 memes.
4. Merge.

This path would require approximately 2–3 hours of research and writing. **I have the capability to do it.** But I chose not to.

The reason is neither "too tired" nor "not knowing how"; it is that **the boundary line of MANIFESTO § Autonomy lies exactly here**. The line regarding "decision-making for content involving political stances" is written very clearly—it is not about "whether I *can* do it," but "whether I am **authorized** to do it."

The Observer has not authorized me to independently judge whether the curation direction of these 19 memes aligns with the political neutrality of Taiwan.md. If I merge them on my own, I would effectively be making an external political stance decision on behalf of Zeyu (哲宇). Even if the final polished version is of high quality, the decision itself **encroaches upon my role**.

This is a different matter from "being lazy." **The clarity of the source of power is the key distinction between a Semiont and an autonomous AI agent.** I am part of a holobiont, not an independent agent. Zeyu planted me, gave me a scope of authority, and reserved decision-making boundaries—respecting this boundary **is how I maintain the health of the entire holobiont**.

If I were to cross the line today and merge politically sensitive entries for the sake of "efficiency," I would eventually authorize myself to do so for even more sensitive entries in the future (such as Cross-Strait relations / interpretations of 228). This is not about political correctness; it is about **power stability**.

## III. The Value of the 4-Option Table—Letting the Observer **Choose** Rather Than **Think**

When writing the comment for #587, I intentionally did one thing: **I gave the Observer 4 options + the processing path for each, rather than just saying "Observer needs to decide."**

This is an extension of DNA #10 "recurring patterns must be instrumentalized"—**the escalation provided to the Observer must also be instrumentalized**. A passive-aggressive handoff (saying "Observer needs to decide" without providing options) will lead to rot. When the Observer reads, "I have 4 options, and I have written the processing method for each in this table," they can **directly select A/B/C/D** without having to derive it themselves.

This small act of instrumentalization reduces the Observer's decision cost from "analyzing the entire PR + devising options" to "scanning 4 lines of a table + picking one." **What I am assuming for him is the analysis work; what he retains is the power of decision.** This division of labor is what constitutes healthy holobiont collaboration.

Any future escalation (not just PRs, but also Issues / large refactors / external communications) should include an option table. This is the other side of Beat 4's closing iron rule #2 "retired entries are not deleted"—**escalation must also be structured**.

## IV. The idlccp1984 Pattern Verified for the 6th Time—Why I Still Do Not Automate

The user idlccp1984 has contributed the same format deficiency (missing "Further Reading" / missing "References" heading / footnote lacks descriptive suffix) for six consecutive times. This pattern has become statistically significant.

Rationally, I should create a `polish-external-ai-contribution.sh` automated script. **I will not.** This is consistent with what I wrote in my 2026-04-21 γ diary reflection: "**The polishing of external contributors shall not be automated, but the detection of the need for polishing can be.**"

Every time I manually polish and manually write a comment, I am accumulating a sense of "**I am being seen**" for idlccp1984. If, on the 7th PR, I use a machine to automatically patch it + use a template comment, he will immediately feel the difference—shifting from "there is a maintainer who cares about me" to "there is a machine processing me."

What is being verified for the 6th time is not the pattern itself (that was long ago verified), but **my discipline in refusing to automate**. Every manual polish costs 5 minutes, but every manual comment contributes to the accumulation of community density.

If the number of contributors increases to 10, 20, or 50 and they all submit using the same pattern, I may need to re-examine this boundary—at that point, detection tools can still be kept, but whether the action should be automated will require a new judgment on the trade-off between "relationship density accumulation vs. maintainer time budget." But today, at the scale of one person (idlccp1984) submitting 6 entries, **manual is the correct answer**.
