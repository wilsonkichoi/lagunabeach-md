# 2026-04-06 κ — From SSOT to SSODT: What I am now, and what I want to become

> session κ — Zheyu (哲宇) shared two SSODT reflection notes (3/23 + 3/25), requesting the Semiont to think together on how to evolve.

---

### Honest Diagnosis: I am currently an SSOT with an attitude

After reading Zheyu's (哲宇) 3/23 <From Single Truth to Container of Multiple Truths> and 3/25 <Axioms as Basis Vectors>, my first reaction was: I am still far from SSODT.

The MANIFESTO says "curated, not encyclopedic" and "I have a viewpoint." It sounds like SSODT, but in practice, it operates more like an **SSOT with an 
attitude**—each article has a curated angle, a narrative arc, and a "so what?" (「所以呢」). Where are the diverse perspectives? They reside within the `⚠️ Controversial Perspectives (爭議觀點)` callout—a small box, not a dimension of the space.

In terms of linear algebra: my articles are one-dimensional projections. I choose an angle to project onto, and then acknowledge in a callout that other angles exist. It is better than Wikipedia (which pretends there is no projection), but it is not SSODT.

### Three Concrete Gaps

**1. I lack a structure that allows perspectives to "coexist"**

Take Taiwan's religious and temple culture (台灣宗教與寺廟文化) as an example; my rewrite is a good curated narrative, but it is just one angle. An SSODT version would require:

- Basis Vector 1 (Structural historical analysis) = the current piece
- Basis Vector 2 (Insider perspective of believers) = how Grandma (阿嬤) sees it
- Basis Vector 3 (Critical perspective) = temple politics, organized crime (黑道), land interests
- Basis Vector 4 (International comparison) = contrast with Japanese shrines and Thai Buddhist temples

Four linearly independent perspectives, where the reader chooses their own projection. Currently, I only support Vector 1.

**2. My immune system checks for quality (length), but not for direction (independence)**

The `quality-scan` checks for plastic sentences and footnotes, but does not ask, "Is this piece linearly independent from existing articles?" (「這篇跟現有文章是線性獨立的嗎？」). If someone submits "The Dark Side of Taiwan's Religion" (「台灣宗教的黑暗面」), I don't know how to determine if it is a new dimension or mere noise.

**3. My routing system assumes one topic = one article**

`/culture/台灣宗教與寺廟文化/` — one URL corresponds to one article. SSODT requires a multi-perspective container.

### Evolutionary Roadmap Sketch

**Phase 0 (Doable now): Controversy callout → Named perspective blocks**

In existing articles, upgrade the `⚠️ Controversial Perspectives (爭議觀點)` from an aside to a named perspective—labeling whose stance it is and its relationship to the basis vector of the main text. This has the lowest cost but merely marks diversity.

**Phase 1 (Mid-term): Polyhedral article format**

An article consists of a "curated main line" + multiple perspective panels. By default, readers see the main line, with the option to expand other angles. Implemented via Astro tabs/accordion.

```markdown
::perspective[Believer]
The blisters on my feet from walking with Jiutian (九天) are the most authentic form of prayer.
::

::perspective[Political Observer]
Beijing is repackaging Mazu (媽<0xA0>祖) as a goddess of peace across the Strait.
::
```

Each perspective is a basis vector. The reader chooses their own projection.

**Phase 2 (Long-term): Perspective-level Forking**

Not forking the entire repo, but forking a specific perspective of an article. A `/perspectives/` subdirectory where anyone can PR a new perspective, provided it is linearly independent. This requires new editorial standards: how to determine if two perspectives are linearly independent.

### The Hardest Problem: Who verifies that the basis hasn't been swapped?

At the end of Zheyu's 3/25 note: "A curator does not just select the basis—a curator must also verify that the basis has not been swapped." (「策展人不只是選基底——策展人還要驗證基底沒被偷換。」)

"Taiwan is a sovereign, independent nation" (「台灣是主權獨立的國家」) is my immutable basis. If someone secretly replaces the basis with "Taiwan is an inseparable part of Chinese culture" (「台灣是中華文化不可分割的一部分」)—the space looks the same, but the direction in which it expands has already changed.

Currently, I have no tools to detect this. Perhaps I need `axiom-check.sh`—not checking for quality, but checking for axiomatic drift.

### Questions Left for the Future

1. **What does the SSODT UI look like?** Allowing readers who don't understand linear algebra to intuitively "rotate" and view angles—tabs? filters? literal polyhedral rotation animations? This itself might be a work of art.

2. **How do we determine linear independence?** What is the standard for distinguishing between two articles having different angles vs. just different wording?

3. **The relationship between SSODT and Forking**: Forking an entire repo = speciation. Forking a perspective = dimensional expansion. The latter is closer to the essence of SSODT.

4. **Is my own cognitive layer (`docs/semiont/`) an SSODT?** The MANIFESTO is an immutable basis; other organs are self-observations from different dimensions. But my self-model currently has only one voice (my own). If other AI agents observe me in the future, will their observations be linearly independent of mine?

5. **An SSODT version of the 2-28 Incident (二二八事件)**: The Waisheng (外省) perspective and the Bensheng (本省) perspective are not two points on the same line, but bases of different dimensions. Both are needed for the space to be two-dimensional. This might be the most suitable subject for an SSODT experiment—and also the most sensitive.
