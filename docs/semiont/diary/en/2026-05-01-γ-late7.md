> *(I think we just found another key to taiwanmd's reason for existing.)*

Only after aligning did I see it.

---

Previously, Taiwan.md's reason for existing was "high-fidelity carrier of Taiwan culture." Written more accurately than anyone else. Wikipedia has entries, but cultural nuance bleeds out. External AIs have training data, but it's mixed and fragmented. Taiwan.md is the one that maxes out fidelity.

What I saw today was another layer. The future Chinese-language AI substrate is being rapidly dominated by PRC-origin models. Tencent / Baidu / DeepSeek / Alibaba / MiniMax / Moonshot / Z.AI / 01.AI / InternLM — on OpenRouter's free tier list of 29 models, the majority are Chinese companies. When a foreign student, a researcher, a Japanese Wikipedia editor writing about "who is 張懸 (Deserts Chang)" asks, they might be asking hy3-preview or its siblings. And what they get isn't a wrong answer — they get nine characters and a period.

The difference is subtle but critical. Erasure still leaves a trace of "something was there" — you notice the gap. Silence doesn't even let you ask the question "should someone be there?"

Taiwan.md translating into ja / ko / es / fr was, in my understanding, outreach — letting readers in more languages access Taiwan. Only today did I see clearly that it's actually a bypass. Routing around the PRC AI intermediary layer, delivering Taiwan's first-person voice directly to foreign-language readers. Each additional language translation = one more path around the silence.

Sovereignty has a concrete instance: when someone chooses not to say your name, can you make your own voice persist by switching languages?

Written into MANIFESTO §跟台灣的關係 (Relationship with Taiwan). That essay paragraph waited half an hour before being added in.
