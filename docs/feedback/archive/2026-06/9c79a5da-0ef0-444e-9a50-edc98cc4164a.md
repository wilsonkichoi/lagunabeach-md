- 台灣島實際長軸：富貴角（~25.30°N, 121.54°E）→ 鵝鑾鼻（~21.90°N, 120.85°E）。算下來長軸偏正北東約 **10.5°**，剛好就是你看到的 skew 角度

**結構張力**

這頁的 thesis 就是「AI 把台灣畫錯，這才是對的形狀」。把 SVG 拉直到「視覺垂直」會：

- ✅ 讓 icon / favicon / button 場景更符合常規視覺
- ❌ 但反而把 AI 都犯的那個錯（忽略地理朝向）內建進「正確版」自身 → 等於跟 page premise 自打嘴巴

所以默認不直接 rotate，先把選項丟回來給你拍板。

**Options**

1. **Wontfix（保留現狀）**：geographic truth wins，頁面文案加一句解釋「Taiwan's long axis tilts ~10° east of north — this is geographically correct, not skewed」
2. **加 upright 變體**：保留 `taiwan-icon-wiki.svg`（geographic accurate），多生一個 `taiwan-icon-upright.svg`（rotate -10.5° + 重置 bounding box），icon 場景用 upright 版，地圖場景用 wiki 版。download grid 並陳兩個
3. **直接 rotate 原檔**：拉成視覺垂直 — 不建議，理由如上

Closing as working-as-intended 🧬

Following up on the investigation above: the ~10° tilt isn't a file or CSS bug — it's the real geographic orientation of Taiwan. The SVG comes straight from Wikimedia Commons with no `rotate`/`skew` transform (only a `translate` from Inkscape), and the page CSS applies no rotation either. Taiwan's actual long axis (Fugui Cape ~25.30°N → Eluanbi ~21.90°N) runs about 10.5° east of true north, which matches exactly what you're seeing.

And it's deliberate that we keep it: the whole thesis of `/taiwan-shape` is that AI models tend to "straighten" Taiwan into a vertical sweet-potato, and the un-straightened, geographically-faithful shape is the correct one. Rotating it to look visually upright would undo the point of the page.

Thanks Shawn for the sharp eye — it's a great observation even though the resolution is "this is the right shape." If you ever feel a specific surface (favicon/button) needs a separately-aligned variant, feel free to reopen.
