> **30-Second Overview**
>
> Chunghwa Telecom is Taiwan's largest telecommunications operator. Since its establishment in 1996, it has undergone a transformation from state-owned monopoly to open competition. 2024 revenues reached NT$230.03 billion, with a market capitalization near NT$1 trillion and approximately 30,000 employees. Not only does it control over 90% of Taiwan's fixed-line market, it is also actively laying out in emerging technology fields such as 5G, cloud computing, and AI — a key driver of Taiwan's digital infrastructure.

## Why It Matters

In the trajectory of Taiwan's economic development, Chunghwa Telecom plays a unique and critical role. It is not only the digital bridge connecting Taiwan to the world, but an important driver witnessing Taiwan's transition from industrial society to information society. From the initial state-owned telecommunications monopoly to today's leading enterprise facing digital transformation challenges, Chunghwa Telecom's development history is a microcosm of the modernization of Taiwan's telecommunications industry.

When we discuss Taiwan's "digital resilience," Chunghwa Telecom's infrastructure network is the physical foundation of that resilience. Whether supporting the broadband network for nationwide remote work during COVID-19, or the infrastructure driving smart city development in the 5G era — Chunghwa Telecom plays an indispensable role.

## Enterprise Overview

Chunghwa Telecom Co., Ltd. was established on July 1, 1996, restructured from the Directorate General of Telecommunications of the Ministry of Transportation and Communications as a state-owned enterprise, completing privatization in 2005. As Taiwan's largest telecommunications operator, Chunghwa Telecom provides comprehensive telecom services including fixed-line, mobile communications, internet, and data communications.

The company's headquarters is located in Xinyi District, Taipei City, with hundreds of service locations across Taiwan. In addition to traditional telecom services, Chunghwa Telecom has in recent years actively transformed, entering cloud computing, information security, IoT, and AI fields, committed to becoming "the best partner for digital transformation."

## Development History: The Metamorphosis from Monopoly to Competition

### State-Owned Period (1996–2005)

Chunghwa Telecom's predecessor can be traced to the Taiwan Power Co. Communications Division established in 1943, later going through the Taiwan Bureau of Telecommunications, Directorate General of Telecommunications, and other phases. On July 1, 1996, to comply with telecommunications liberalization policy, the Directorate General of Telecommunications was restructured into Chunghwa Telecom Co., Ltd. as a state-owned enterprise.

During this period, Chunghwa Telecom enjoyed a fixed-line monopoly as Taiwan's sole provider of telecom services. Although lacking competitive pressure, the company used this period to heavily invest in infrastructure, laying a solid foundation for subsequent open competition.

### Open Competition Period (2005–2010)

Privatization was completed in August 2005, with government shareholding dropping to 35.36%. With competitors such as Taiwan Mobile and FarEasTone entering the market, Chunghwa Telecom faced unprecedented competitive pressure.

During this period, Chunghwa Telecom experienced the identity transformation from "monopolist" to "competitor." The company began paying attention to service quality, customer experience, and innovation capability, gradually establishing market-oriented management thinking.

### Digital Transformation Period (2010–Present)

Facing the proliferation of smartphones, the rise of digital native generations, and the development of the digital economy, Chunghwa Telecom launched comprehensive digital transformation. Upgrading from a pure "pipe provider" to a "digital service integrator."

In recent years, with the rise of emerging technologies such as 5G, AI, and IoT, Chunghwa Telecom has not only invested in next-generation network infrastructure but is also actively developing enterprise-level solutions, successfully transforming from a traditional telecom operator to a comprehensive ICT service provider.

## Global Influence: Taiwan's Bridge to the World

### International Submarine Cable Network

Chunghwa Telecom has invested in and participated in the construction of multiple international submarine fiber optic cables, including APCN-2, SEA-ME-WE 3, and the Trans-Pacific Express (TPE) cable systems. These investments not only ensure stable connectivity between Taiwan and the world, but also make Taiwan an important digital hub in the Asia-Pacific region.

### Enterprise Overseas Footprint

Chunghwa Telecom has established subsidiaries or offices in the United States, Japan, Vietnam, Thailand, and other locations, providing local telecom services for Taiwanese businesses and helping international enterprises enter the Taiwan market. Particularly in Southeast Asia, Chunghwa Telecom's experience and technology exports have become an important manifestation of Taiwan's soft power.

### Technical Standards Participation

As Taiwan's representative, Chunghwa Telecom actively participates in the formulation of international telecom standards, including 5G, IoT, AI, and other emerging technology fields. This participation not only elevates Taiwan's voice in the international telecom community but also ensures Taiwan's technological development stays aligned with international standards.

## Innovation Capability: Leading Taiwan's Digital Transformation

### 5G Ecosystem Building

Chunghwa Telecom is not only one of the first recipients of Taiwan's 5G licenses, but is also actively building a 5G ecosystem. From smart manufacturing and smart healthcare to smart cities, Chunghwa Telecom works with industry partners to develop various 5G application scenarios, driving the digital transformation of Taiwan's industries.

### AI and Cloud Services

Through its self-built cloud data centers and AI platforms, Chunghwa Telecom provides enterprise customers with one-stop digital transformation solutions. Its AI assistant "smart customer service" and cloud ERP systems have become important tools for Taiwan's SMEs' digitalization.

### Cybersecurity Protection System

Facing increasingly severe network security threats, Chunghwa Telecom has established a comprehensive cybersecurity protection system, not only protecting its own network security but also providing cybersecurity services to government agencies and enterprise customers. Its cybersecurity service annual growth of 56% shows the strong market demand for professional cybersecurity services.

## Social Responsibility: Bridging the Digital Divide

### Rural Broadband Construction

Even when commercial benefits are limited, Chunghwa Telecom continues to invest in broadband construction in rural areas, ensuring that urban-rural digital gaps do not widen. This "universal service" spirit reflects the social responsibility sense derived from its state-owned enterprise origins.

### Digital Education Promotion

Through the "HiNet Digital Learning" platform and various digital literacy courses, Chunghwa Telecom helps citizens adapt to the digital age. Especially during COVID-19, it provided free online teaching resources to support nationwide distance learning needs.

### Environmental Sustainability Development

Chunghwa Telecom has committed to achieving carbon neutrality by 2030, and is actively investing in green data centers, renewable energy, and other environmental facilities. As the operator of Taiwan's largest telecom infrastructure, its environmental efforts have a model effect on the entire industry.

## Challenges Faced

### Traditional Business Decline

As communication software becomes widespread and lifestyles change, traditional local telephone and long-distance call businesses continue to decline. Although these businesses are still a stable revenue source for Chunghwa Telecom, the long-term trend is difficult to reverse.

### 5G Investment Pressure

5G network construction requires large capital investment, but business models are still being explored. How to maintain profitability while investing in 5G infrastructure is a major challenge for Chunghwa Telecom.

### Talent Transformation Needs

Transitioning from traditional telecom technology to new fields such as AI, cloud computing, and cybersecurity requires large numbers of employees with new skills. How to cultivate existing employees' new skills while attracting external talent to join tests the company's human resources strategy.

### Intensifying Competition

In addition to competition from traditional telecom peers, cloud services from international tech giants such as Google and Amazon, and emerging OTT service operators, all pose threats to Chunghwa Telecom's business.

## Future Prospects

### AI-First Strategy

Chunghwa Telecom announced that 2025 will "activate the future with AI," planning to introduce AI technology into all business processes. From customer service and network operations to new product development, AI will become the core of competitive advantage.

### Ecosystem Collaboration Model

No longer operating alone, but establishing deep cooperative relationships with industry partners. Through open platforms and APIs, allowing more operators to develop innovative services based on Chunghwa Telecom's infrastructure, forming a mutually beneficial ecosystem.

### International Market Expansion

Drawing on digital transformation experience accumulated in Taiwan, Chunghwa Telecom plans to export successful models to overseas markets such as Southeast Asia, becoming a regional ICT service provider.

### Sustainable Development Goals

Beyond commercial success, Chunghwa Telecom also lists sustainable development as a core goal. From carbon neutrality commitments to digital inclusivity, the company hopes to generate positive impacts on society and the environment while pursuing profits.

Chunghwa Telecom's story is an important microcosm of Taiwan's transition from agricultural society to digital society. In this rapidly changing digital era, this veteran enterprise that has witnessed Taiwan's communications development is embracing the challenges and opportunities of the future with a new posture.
