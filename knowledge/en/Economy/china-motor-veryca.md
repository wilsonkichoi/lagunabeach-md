> **30-second overview:**
> The CMC Veryca is not merely a commercial vehicle; it is synonymous with Taiwan's "fortune van" culture. The Varica, launched in 1988, laid the foundation with a market share as high as 80 percent, and the Veryca, which took over in 2000, extended that territory, long dominating the light commercial vehicle market. The history of this vehicle condenses the struggles and breakthroughs of Taiwan's automobile industry as it moved from "technology importation" to "independent development." Renamed J Space in 2024 for a fresh start, it is attempting to continue carrying the entrepreneurial dreams of countless Taiwanese families amid the waves of electrification and safety regulation.

"Dad! Can this van really make us rich?" In Taiwan's traditional markets and industrial districts, this small blue truck is almost the first member of every entrepreneurial family. It is affectionately called the "fortune van," a name that does not come from expensive equipment, but from the resilience with which it carried goods through streets and alleys during Taiwan's economic transformation.

### Core Tension: Japanese Backbone, Taiwanese Soul

The origins of the CMC Veryca can be traced to the Mitsubishi-licensed Minicab. Yet the reason the Veryca became Taiwan's "legendary vehicle" was not that it was a perfect Japanese car, but that it was "not original-spec, but born for Taiwan." Japan's K-Car specifications often proved ill-suited to Taiwan's steep slopes, high temperatures, and overloaded operating environments.

1. **A turning point in independent development**: In 1988, China Motor invested NT$1.4 billion to independently develop the CMC Varica. At the time, this was Taiwan's most important attempt at independent automobile development after the Yulon Feeling 101 [^1].
2. **Localized evolution of specifications**: The Veryca upgraded the original Japanese Minicab's small engine from 1.2L to later 1.3L and 1.5L versions, and even developed a four-wheel-drive (4WD) variant to cope with the harsh terrain of Taiwan's mountain tea plantations and orchards [^4].
3. **Resilience for survival**: In the era when Taiwan joined the WTO and faced challenges from imported vehicles, the Veryca built a "repair wall" through extremely high parts commonality and ease of maintenance, making it possible for almost any roadside scooter repair shop to handle its basic repairs.

📝 Curator's note: The Veryca was not designed in a laboratory. It was honed on Taiwan's steep orchard slopes and in flooded market streets.

### From Minicab to Veryca: A Vehicle for the Economic Miracle

The history of Taiwan's commercial vehicle development is almost a genealogy of China Motor. In 1978, the third-generation Minicab was localized for domestic production as the "CMC Minicab," beginning China Motor's long-term cooperation with Mitsubishi [^3]. The subsequent Varica and Veryca then carried forward the map of Taiwan's light commercial vehicle market.

| Model           | Production Years | Key Position                                                                                                                                                         |
| :-------------- | :--------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **CMC Minicab** | 1978 - 1988      | China Motor's first commercial vehicle, opening Taiwan's domestically produced light commercial market                                                               |
| **CMC Varica**  | 1988 - 2000      | Taiwan's first independently developed commercial vehicle backed by NT$1.4 billion in investment; captured 80 percent market share within three years of launch [^5] |
| **CMC Veryca**  | 2000 - 2024      | Took over from the Varica and long dominated the light commercial vehicle market [^2]                                                                                |
| **J Space**     | 2024 - present   | The Veryca's major facelift and renaming, with strengthened safety and driver-assistance features [^7]                                                               |

### Challenges and Controversies: The Monopolist's Arrogance and Awakening

For a long time, the Veryca had almost no competitors in Taiwan, which also led to controversy over its slow equipment updates. Many owners criticized its insufficient safety equipment and outdated chassis technology. It was not until 2022, when the Toyota Town Ace made a strong entry into Taiwan, that the Veryca's two-decade monopoly was broken.

"This was overdue competition," automotive critics noted. The arrival of the Town Ace forced China Motor to launch the heavily updated **J Space** in 2024, not only raising power output to 107 horsepower, but also introducing a Level 2 automated driving assistance system for the first time [^7]. This competition benefited consumers and also proved that Taiwan's commercial vehicle market was being forced to transform under the pressure of safety regulations.

### Cultural Resonance: More Than Transportation

The Veryca occupies a special place in Taiwanese culture. It has appeared not only in drifting performances by racing driver Feng Ren-chih [^6], but also in countless films and literary works, symbolizing the spirit of grassroots struggle.

In 2010, under the leadership of the Department of Industrial Technology at the Ministry of Economic Affairs, the Veryca also became a pioneer in Taiwan's development of light electric commercial vehicles [^4]. Although the road to electrification has been long, this small blue vehicle has always tried to adapt to new eras.

The Veryca's value lies not in where its transmission comes from, but in the sense of security it gives countless entrepreneurs when it starts up at four in the morning.
