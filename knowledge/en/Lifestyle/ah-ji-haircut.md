# Ah-Ji Haircut

The "Ah-Ji Haircut" (阿志頭) is a specific term for a male hairstyle that has risen in Taiwanese internet culture in recent years. It is not just a fashion choice but a cultural symbol with strong meme characteristics and social labels.

## Visual Features

Technically, the Ah-Ji Haircut is a variation of the "Edgar cut," with its core features including:

- **High Shaved Sides and Back**: Using clippers to shave the sides and back short, forming a sharp Undercut.
- **Perfectly Straight Horizontal Fringe**: The top retains thick hair, with the fringe grown long and deliberately combed down to cover the forehead, cut into an extremely straight horizontal line or slight arc. From a distance, it looks like a precise cap covering the head.
- **High-Recognition Silhouette**: The edges of the hair are usually trimmed very sharply, forming obvious right angles or geometric outlines.

## Origin and Meme Culture

The name "Ah-Ji Haircut" originates from an internet meme widely circulated in Taiwan, usually titled "Ah-Ji from the class next door" (隔壁班的阿志). The character in the image has a highly recognizable hairstyle. Due to the viral spread of this image on social media and forums (such as PTT and Dcard), netizens began to jokingly refer to this style as the "Ah-Ji Haircut"[^1].

## Social Labels and "8+9" Culture

In Taiwanese social perception, the Ah-Ji Haircut is often linked to the "8+9" subculture (referring to young men with lower socio-economic status or those associated with temple and gang cultures). This labeling phenomenon reflects public stereotypes about specific outfits and hairstyles:

1.  **Subcultural Identity**: For some young people, this hairstyle is easy to maintain, affordable (often done at 100 NTD quick cut shops), and serves a group identification function with a "brotherhood" vibe.
2.  **Social Bias and Police Checks**: Due to the strong stereotype, netizens often share that police may increase the frequency of checks based on the visible subcultural traits associated with this hairstyle[^2].
3.  **Meme-like Satire**: In the eyes of most netizens, the Ah-Ji Haircut is mostly treated as a joke or an aesthetic irony, becoming a meme language that crosses social classes.

## Conclusion

The popularity of the Ah-Ji Haircut proves that in the internet age, a hairstyle can transcend pure aesthetics to become a complex carrier of social class, subcultural identity, and online humor. Regardless of its appraisal, it has become an undeniable slice of contemporary Taiwanese pop culture.
