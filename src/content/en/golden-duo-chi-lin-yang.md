# Wang Chi-Lin and Lee Yang (The Golden Duo)

> **30-second overview:** On July 31, 2021, badminton men's doubles "Golden Duo" took just 34 minutes in the Tokyo Olympics final to defeat the Chinese pair 21-18, 21-12 in straight sets, winning Taiwan's first Olympic gold medal in badminton. From middle school classmates to world champions, they successfully defended their title at the 2024 Paris Olympics.

7:30 PM, July 31, 2021, at the Musashino Forest Sport Plaza in Tokyo. The match timer stopped at 34 minutes.

Wang Chi-Lin delivered a powerful smash, and the Chinese pair Li Jun-hui and Liu Yu-chen called for a Hawk-Eye challenge. The result showed: in! The score was fixed at 21-18, 21-12.

Two Taiwanese boys embraced and wept, while 23 million Taiwanese erupted in cheers simultaneously. This was not just Taiwan's first Olympic gold medal in badminton, but a legendary story written by two middle school classmates.

## The Starting Point of Two Parallel Lines

### Wang Chi-Lin: The Attacker from Taipei

Wang Chi-Lin, born January 18, 1995, in Taipei City. Standing 185 cm tall, he moves like a tower on the badminton court. He didn't come from a badminton family, but showed exceptional athletic talent.

His playing style is famous for its violent aesthetics: smash speeds often exceed 320 km/h, with that oppressive attack instilling fear in opponents. During his youth, Wang Chi-Lin was the most destructive attacker among peers, with coaches all recognizing him as a natural finisher.

### Lee Yang: The Technical Master from a Badminton Family

Lee Yang, born May 13, 1995, in Taipei City, registered in Kinmen County's Jinning Township. He's a standard second-generation badminton family member—his father Li Jun-yu was a former national badminton player who took Lee Yang and his siblings to practice at Zhongtong Temple in Zhonghe from a young age.

Lee Yang initially played handball before switching to badminton. Unlike Wang Chi-Lin's violent aesthetics, Lee Yang took the technical route: excellent touch, delicate net play, and outstanding court reading ability. He excels at using finesse to neutralize opponent attacks and can create deadly attacking opportunities at crucial moments.

### Meeting as Middle School Classmates

Their badminton connection began at Taipei Municipal Zhongshan Junior High School. They were classmates in middle school but not partners. At the time, they followed different development trajectories: Wang Chi-Lin focused on attacking techniques while Lee Yang worked on technical details.

In his senior year, Lee Yang almost gave up badminton because he couldn't advance to the first division, choosing to study business administration at Taipei University of Business. But in the second semester, he picked up the racket again, and their destinies began to intertwine.

## Birth of the Golden Duo: Chemical Reaction

### The 2017 Attempt

In 2017, the coaching team began trying different partnership combinations. Wang Chi-Lin had previously partnered with Chen Hong-lin to form the "Double Lin Combination" (also called "Qilin Pair"), reaching a highest world ranking of fourth. But to seek better pairing effects, coaches decided to let Wang Chi-Lin and Lee Yang try together.

Merging two completely different playing styles wasn't easy. Wang Chi-Lin was used to controlling match rhythm from the backcourt, while Lee Yang needed sufficient space to display his technique in the frontcourt. The coaching team spent months adjusting their positioning and rotation methods.

### Perfecting Tactical Division

After long-term adaptation, the "Golden Duo" developed a unique tactical system:

**Attack-Defense Transition**: Lee Yang handles frontcourt defense and transitions, resolving crises and seeking counter-attack opportunities; once opportunities arise, Wang Chi-Lin immediately takes over offensive rhythm, using powerful smashes to finish opponents.

**Psychological Understanding**: Wang Chi-Lin once said: "I know when Lee Yang will give way to the shuttle, and he knows from which angle I'm preparing to smash." This nearly telepathic understanding became key to their victories.

**Technical Complementarity**: Wang Chi-Lin's smashes are not only powerful but increasingly precise; Lee Yang's net techniques become more delicate, creating angles and spins that surprise opponents.

## Tokyo Olympics: The 34-Minute Miracle

### Breaking Out of the Group of Death

At the Tokyo Olympics, the Golden Duo participated as the third-ranked pair worldwide. But they were placed in the "Group of Death," losing their first group match against the Indonesian pair and almost failing to advance.

But this defeat awakened their fighting spirit. In their final two group matches, they adjusted tactics and ultimately advanced to the knockout stage as group runners-up.

### Knockout Stage Comeback

**Quarterfinal vs Malaysia**: Won after losing the first set, coming back to win 2-1
**Semifinal vs Indonesia's Ahsan/Setiawan**: Facing the world's #1 pair, they reversed to win 2-1
**Final vs China's Li Jun-hui/Liu Yu-chen**: Dominated in straight sets to win gold

Throughout the knockout stage, the Golden Duo defeated all top-four seeds, becoming the first unseeded gold medalists in Olympic badminton men's doubles history.

### Perfect Performance in the Final

On the evening of July 31, 2021, facing the Chinese "Twin Towers" Li Jun-hui and Liu Yu-chen, the Golden Duo delivered a dominant performance:

**First Set**: 21-18 victory, showing steady rhythm control
**Second Set**: 21-12 big win, completely suppressing opponents

The entire match took just 34 minutes, creating one of the fastest Olympic badminton final finishes on record. On the final point, Lee Yang's backhand return landed in, the Chinese pair challenged with Hawk-Eye, and the result was ruled in—Taiwan's first Olympic gold medal in badminton was born.

## Paris Olympics: The Glory of Defense

### 2024's Return to Battle

At the 2024 Paris Olympics, the 29-year-old Golden Duo faced greater challenges. Increasing age, injury troubles, young opponents' impact—everyone wondered if they could recreate the miracle from three years ago.

But the Golden Duo proved with their strength that experience and understanding are the best weapons. They won gold again at the Paris Olympics, becoming Taiwan's first Olympic champions to successfully defend their title in badminton.

### Lee Yang's Retirement

After the Paris Olympics, Lee Yang announced his retirement. He had revealed before the competition: "I had retirement feelings at 18, and now at 29, I've played 11 extra years." Now he wants to pass on his experience to young players.

Wang Chi-Lin continues his badminton career, forming a new "Lin-Qi Pair" with a new partner, continuing to compete on the international stage.

## Technical Characteristics and Tactical Analysis

### Wang Chi-Lin's Attacking Threat

Wang Chi-Lin's smashes are the Golden Duo's biggest weapon:

- **Speed**: Maximum over 320 km/h
- **Placement**: High precision, skilled at attacking opponent gaps
- **Timing**: Coordination with Lee Yang allows him to attack from optimal positions

### Lee Yang's Technical Versatility

Lee Yang's technical characteristics lie in versatility:

- **Net Techniques**: Delicate push-block and rescue abilities
- **Defensive Skills**: Excellent reaction speed and judgment
- **Tactical Awareness**: Can create optimal attacking opportunities for his partner

### Evolution of Partnership Understanding

Their understanding is built on long-term training:

- **Silent Communication**: Rarely need verbal communication during matches
- **Space Allocation**: Extremely precise division of court positions
- **Attack-Defense Transitions**: Can complete role switches instantly

## Significance and Impact

### Taiwan Badminton's Breakthrough

The Golden Duo's success holds epochal significance for Taiwan badminton:

- **First Olympic Gold**: Ended Taiwan badminton's 60-year Olympic wait
- **Men's Doubles Breakthrough**: Proved Taiwan has more than just Tai Tzu-ying's women's singles
- **Inspirational Effect**: Boosted grassroots badminton development, more young people took up badminton

### Sports Spirit Exemplar

Their story embodies important sporting values:

- **Teamwork**: Individual excellence + team coordination = championship
- **Perseverance**: 20 years of persistence from middle school classmates to Olympic champions
- **Mutual Benefit**: People with different qualities can create greater value together

### Social and Cultural Impact

The Golden Duo's success transcended sports:

- **National Heroes**: Became household sports stars across Taiwan
- **Role Model Legacy**: Actively participate in grassroots promotion and teaching
- **Friendship Example**: Demonstrated true friendship and partnership

After retirement, Lee Yang engages in:

- **Practice Support**: Continuing to support Taiwan badminton in different forms
- **Family Legacy**: Father Li Jun-yu still competes in senior tournaments, the whole family loves badminton

### Wang Chi-Lin's New Partner

Wang Chi-Lin continues competing with a new partner:

- **New Lin-Qi Pair**: Forms new combination with new teammate
- **Experience Transfer**: Applies coordination experience with Lee Yang to new combination
- **Continuing Goals**: Continues striving for Taiwan badminton glory on international stage

## Conclusion: A Legend of Friendship and Glory

The story of the "Golden Duo" is essentially about friendship, dreams, and perseverance. Two middle school classmates used 20 years to elevate their friendship into a globally watched badminton legend.

They proved that: people with different personalities can create perfect partnerships, long-term trust and coordination can overcome talent, and Taiwan's young people can shine on the world's highest stage.

When that historic moment was frozen on July 31, 2021, the "Golden Duo" not only won Taiwan's first Olympic gold medal in badminton but also wrote the best footnote for all dreamers: as long as you persist long enough and coordinate well enough, miracles will happen.

Even though Lee Yang has retired, the name "Golden Duo" will forever be linked with the greatest moment in Taiwan badminton history. That perfect 34-minute performance will be remembered forever.
