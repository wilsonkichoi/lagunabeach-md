> **30-Second Overview**
>
> Taiwan Cement Corporation was founded in 1946 and became Taiwan's first listed company in 1962, with stock code 1101 now a legend of the Taiwan stock market. After transitioning from state to private ownership and being led across three generations by Koo Chen-fu, Koo Cheng-yung, and Chang An-ping, its 2024 revenue was NT$154.6 billion. From traditional cement industry it has successfully transformed into a "green environmental engineering company," opening new tracks in green energy, energy storage, and the circular economy in the face of global pressure to reduce carbon.

## A Stock Exchange Born for One Company

February 9, 1962 — a day that rewrote Taiwan's economic history. The Taiwan Stock Exchange officially opened for business, and the first stock listed was Taiwan Cement, with code 1101.

The significance of this "001" goes far beyond the number itself. Many people do not know that the Taiwan Stock Exchange was established to a large extent specifically to list Taiwan Cement. At that time, Taiwan was just beginning to promote a capital market and needed a company of sufficient scale and reputation to serve as a "lead horse" — and Taiwan Cement was the ideal choice.

From that point on, 1101 became not merely a stock code but a symbol of the origins of Taiwan's capital market. Whenever people discuss the history of the Taiwan stock market, they begin with this number. Taiwan Cement is not just an enterprise — it is a witness to the birth of Taiwan's modern financial markets.

## The Historic Shareholders' Meeting in Front of the Presidential Office

November 11, 1954 at 11:11 a.m. — thousands gathered at the Armed Forces Sports Field in front of Taipei's Presidential Office. This was not a military ceremony but the first shareholders' meeting following Taiwan Cement's privatization.

The timing — chosen as the 11th day of the 11th month at 11:11 — and the location in front of the Presidential Office were not coincidences. The political atmosphere of the time gave everything a symbolic weight: Taiwan Cement's privatization represented a major transformation of Taiwan's economic system.

The shareholders' meeting scene was essentially a gathering of Taiwan's major political and business figures. Lin Po-shou of the Banqiao Lin family was elected as the first chairman; Koo Chen-fu of the Lukang Koo family served as managing director; Wu San-lien of the Tainan faction, the Wu family of Shin Kong Group, the Lin family of Wufeng, the Yan family of Keelung — almost all of Taiwan's major families participated in investing in Taiwan Cement.

This arrangement had deep significance. The government needed respected families to support Taiwan Cement's privatization, while these families saw the opportunity of Taiwan's economic takeoff. One might say that Taiwan Cement's shareholders' meeting was a microcosm of the postwar Taiwanese political and business structure.

## Koo Chen-fu's Thirty-Year Legend

In 1973, Koo Chen-fu, backed by his outstanding political and business connections and the support of all the major families, formally assumed the chairmanship of Taiwan Cement. He held this position for 30 years — encompassing Taiwan Cement's most critical period of expansion.

Koo Chen-fu was an entrepreneur with vision. He understood that pure cement manufacturing had its limits, and that diversification was necessary. Under his leadership, Taiwan Cement transformed from a simple building material manufacturer into a diversified enterprise group, extending its business into petrochemicals, electronics, finance, real estate, and other fields.

Most impressively was Koo Chen-fu's investment acumen. He invested in Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company, Taiwan High Speed Rail, Han Shen Department Store, and other major enterprises — hardly ever misjudging a trend. This investment capability was not luck but a deep understanding of Taiwan's economic pulse.

More importantly was his international positioning. In the 1980s, Taiwan Cement began investing in mainland China, becoming an important bridge for cross-strait economic and trade exchange. This decision was controversial at the time but proved highly far-sighted in hindsight.

## Succession from Father to Son

In 2003, Koo Cheng-yung took over as chairman, opening Taiwan Cement's second-generation leadership period. Compared with his father, Koo Cheng-yung placed greater emphasis on technological innovation and environmental development.

Under his leadership, Taiwan Cement invested heavily in environmental protection equipment, promoting clean production technology. More importantly, Koo Cheng-yung began reflecting on the question of sustainable development for the cement industry — how to meet construction needs while reducing the impact on the environment.

Koo Cheng-yung passed away unexpectedly in 2017, ending his 14-year leadership. But his efforts toward environmental transformation laid the groundwork for Taiwan Cement's later green transition.

## Chang An-ping's Green Revolution

In 2017, Koo Cheng-yung's brother-in-law Chang An-ping took over as chairman and put forward a vision that shook the industry: transforming Taiwan Cement from a "cement manufacturer" into a "green environmental engineering company."

This decision required enormous courage. Cement was Taiwan Cement's core business; undertaking such a fundamental transformation meant challenging its own core competitiveness. But Chang An-ping understood that, in the face of the global carbon-neutral trend, the traditional cement industry had to find a new development path.

In May 2024, Taiwan Cement formally established its "four-legged" strategy: Taiwan and mainland China cement businesses, European and African low-carbon cement, new energy businesses, and resource recycling. This framework clearly shows Taiwan Cement's determination to transform — from single-product cement manufacturing to diversified green services.

## The Transformation Results Behind NT$154.6 Billion

In 2024, Taiwan Cement delivered outstanding results: revenue of NT$154.6 billion and net profit of NT$15.6 billion. More importantly, a considerable proportion of this result came from green energy and environmental protection businesses, indicating that the transformation strategy is beginning to bear fruit.

Taiwan Cement's transformation is not empty talk — it is a genuinely concrete business positioning. Through Heping Power developing wind power, through ENPOWER developing energy storage technology, through Daho Environmental promoting the circular economy... these subsidiaries have become new growth engines for Taiwan Cement.

Particularly worth noting is the energy storage business. As the share of renewable energy increases, energy storage has become a critical link in the power system. Taiwan Cement is competing in this market through ENPOWER, and has already secured an important position in Taiwan's energy storage industry.

## The Circular Economy Magic of the Cement Kiln

Taiwan Cement's most distinctive competitive advantage is the transformation of cement kilns into core equipment of the circular economy.

Traditionally, cement kilns were simply tools for firing cement. But Taiwan Cement discovered that high-temperature cement kilns can cooperatively treat various types of waste — converting waste into raw materials or fuel for cement production. This "co-processing" technology allows Taiwan Cement to earn revenue from waste treatment while reducing the use of natural resources.

This innovative model is already operating successfully at the Heping plant in Hualien. Waste tires, wastewater treatment sludge, construction waste... various wastes are "reborn" as useful resources in the cement kiln's high temperatures. This is not merely technological innovation — it is also business model innovation.

## Challenges and Opportunities in the Carbon-Neutral Era

The cement industry is a carbon-intensive sector. Facing the 2050 carbon-neutral target, Taiwan Cement confronts unprecedented challenges. But crises are often also turning points.

Taiwan Cement's response strategy is multi-pronged: developing low-carbon cement technology, investing in carbon capture technology, increasing the proportion of alternative fuels used, expanding renewable energy positioning... These efforts are beginning to show results; Taiwan Cement's receipt of the Net-Zero Industrial Competitiveness Award in 2024 is the best proof.

More importantly, Taiwan Cement does not treat carbon neutrality as a burden but views it as a transformation opportunity. Through the development and application of green technology, Taiwan Cement is shifting from traditional manufacturing toward technical services — from cost center to profit center.

## 76 Years Witnessing Taiwan's Construction Miracle

From its founding in 1946 to the present, Taiwan Cement has used 76 years to witness Taiwan's construction miracle. Taiwan's freeways, MRT systems, Taipei 101, Taoyuan Airport... almost all major construction projects have used Taiwan Cement's products.

More importantly, Taiwan Cement's development trajectory is a microcosm of Taiwan's economic development: from postwar reconstruction to economic takeoff, from traditional manufacturing to high-tech industry, from the domestic market to international competition — Taiwan Cement has been an important participant and driver throughout.

As Taiwan's first listed company, Taiwan Cement has also witnessed the development of Taiwan's capital market. From the stock code 1101 in 1962 to the Taiwan stock market's record highs today, Taiwan Cement has always been an important member of Taiwan's securities market.

## The Traditional Industry's Dazzling Reinvention

Taiwan Cement's story tells us: there are no sunset industries, only sunset thinking. Faced with changing times, the key is not to resist change but to proactively embrace transformation.

From Chang An-ping's green revolution we can see that traditional industries can still find new positioning in the new era. The key is having the courage to transform, the ability to innovate, and accurate judgment of future trends.

In today's world of universal pursuit of sustainable development, Taiwan Cement's transformation experience holds important reference value for other traditional industries. It proves that even an "old" industry like cement can — through technological innovation and business model innovation — find renewed vitality in a new era context.

The legend of Taiwan's first listed company continues to be written, and this time the theme is green, sustainable, and innovative.
