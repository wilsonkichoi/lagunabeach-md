## VERIFIED FACTS

### 1. Birth & Identity

- **Born:** November 26, 1993
- **Birthplace:** Luodong Township (羅東鎮), Yilan County — NOT Taitung
  - IMPORTANT: Family heritage is from the Malan Amis tribe (馬蘭部落) in Taitung County, but she was born in Yilan
- **Amis name:** Tana
- **Ethnicity:** Amis Aboriginal people (阿美族)
- **Birth complications:** Breech position + umbilical cord around neck; 10+ hour labour at Yilan Holy Mother Hospital
- **Name meaning:** "Hsing-Chun" (婞淳) — her Mandarin given name 倖存 alludes to "survived by luck"; family gave the name wishing her abundance and happiness
- **Family:** Single-parent household; raised by grandmother; mother worked away from home. Eldest daughter in an Amis matrilineal society.
- **Education:** National Taitung University Affiliated Physical Education Senior High School (台東大學附屬體育中學); Fu Jen Catholic University (輔仁大學), Department of Physical Education
- **Notable relatives:** Great-grandmother Lu Jingzi (盧靜子), Golden Melody Award winner; uncle Lu Xueyi (盧學叡)

**Sources:** Wikipedia ZH, Wikipedia EN, Mirror Media

### 2. Discovery & Coaching

**Early discovery (蒲雅玲 / Pu Ya-ling):**

- Initially excelled at basketball and track & field in junior high school
- At a school sports meet, dropped the baton in a relay — "devastated"
- Same day won gold in weightlifting; recognized talent
- At the 2009 National Secondary School Sports Games (全國中等學校運動會) won gold in women's 53kg — approximately age 15
- Entered Taitung Physical Education High School (台東體中) and met mentor **蒲雅玲 (Pu Ya-ling)**, who coached her initial specialization in weightlifting
- Upon winning Tokyo gold in 2021, Pu Ya-ling wept watching from Taitung and said: "我們等這塊金牌等了好多年，2012年、2016年到現在" ("We waited so many years for this gold, from 2012, to 2016, and now")

**Coach 林敬能 (Lin Jing-neng):**

- Took over coaching when Kuo was approximately 17 years old
- Lin Jing-neng has stated: "我從17歲帶郭婞淳比賽" ("I have coached Kuo since she was 17")
- This puts the start of their collaboration around 2010–2011, when she joined the national training program
- Taiwan Panorama reports: "Met Coach Lin Geng Neng while studying at Affiliated Physical Education Senior High School of National Taitung University"
- After 2.5 years of training together: breakthrough year 2013 with four gold medals
- As of 2021, they had worked together for approximately 11 years

**NOTE:** The old article may have cited 林敬能 as discovering her. This is INACCURATE. Her first discovery and initial mentoring was by 蒲雅玲. Lin Jing-neng became her senior national-level coach around age 17. There is NO verified account of Lin "discovering" her.

**Sources:** Mirror Media, ETtoday (蒲雅玲 reunion), CWant, Wikipedia ZH

### 3. 2014 Injury — VERIFIED DETAILS

**IMPORTANT CORRECTION:** The injury was NOT to her elbow (手肘). It was to her RIGHT THIGH.

- **Date:** May 2014, during training in preparation for the 2014 Asian Games
- **Body part:** Right thigh — specifically the **vastus lateralis** (股外側肌), the outer quadriceps muscle
- **Severity:** **70–80% muscle tear** (sources vary: Wikipedia ZH says 80%; multiple news reports say 70%; most recent check of Wikipedia ZH confirms "右大腿股外側肌…80%斷裂")
  - Recommend citing 70–80% rupture, or "nearly complete tear" to be safe
- **Cause:** A 141kg barbell fell on/crushed her right thigh during training at the National Training Center (國家訓練中心)
- **Immediate impact:** Could not walk; required wheelchair for approximately two months
- **Recovery duration:** Four months rehabilitation (Taiwan Panorama). Could not lift the weights she previously handled easily; developed psychological fear of lifting.
- **Psychological breakthrough:** After approximately one year of rehabilitation, during a 7-hour training session, she successfully lifted 100kg again
- **Competition result after injury:** 2014 Asian Games (Incheon) — finished 4th in women's 58kg with 219kg total. November 2014 World Championships — 6th with 223kg total.

**Sources:** Taiwan Panorama, ETtoday sports.ettoday.net/news/2041280, Wikipedia ZH, Mirror Media, Yahoo Sports Taiwan

#### 2021 Tokyo Olympics (July 27, 2021)

- Weight class: 59kg
- Snatch: **103kg — OLYMPIC RECORD**
- Clean & Jerk: **133kg — OLYMPIC RECORD**
- Total: **236kg — OLYMPIC RECORD**

- Note: These were Olympic records, NOT world records (her own world records were much higher: snatch 110, C&J 140, total 247)

- Result: **BRONZE MEDAL**
- Gold: China's Luo Shifang — 241kg (new Olympic record)
- Silver: Canada's Maude Charron — 236kg (1kg ahead of Kuo)
- Historical significance: First Taiwanese athlete (male or female) to win medals at THREE consecutive Olympics
- Competition date was Taiwan's Father's Day
- Source: Focus Taiwan, Taiwan News, Taipei Times, TVBS

## NARRATIVE HOOK CANDIDATES

1. **Birth as metaphor:** She barely survived birth (cord around neck, breach, 10+ hour labor at Yilan Holy Mother Hospital) — the same hospital she would one day try to donate an ambulance to. Life and near-death bookending.

2. **The dropped baton:** A clumsy relay-race stumble in junior high school inadvertently launched one of Asia's greatest athletic careers. She lost at running; she won everything at lifting.

3. **The ambulance calculation:** Lying on the floor of the National Training Center in May 2014, right thigh muscle 70–80% torn by a 141kg bar, waiting for an ambulance she thought was taking too long — and thinking not of herself but of the villages back in Taitung with no hospital nearby. The ambulance she donated in January 2016 is the direct result.

4. **Tokyo weight of 236kg vs. world record 247kg:** She walked into Tokyo holding three world records (snatch 110, C&J 140, total 247), won gold with only 236kg, and then failed at 141kg on her last attempt. She never touched her own world record in competition. The gap between what she could do and what the Olympics required is its own story.

5. **Three Olympics, two bronzes and one gold:** 2016 Rio = bronze. 2020 Tokyo = gold. 2024 Paris = bronze, with a bad back, at age 30, still 1kg from the silver. The arc of a career measured in kilograms.

6. **Great-grandmother the singer:** She lifts iron; her great-grandmother Lu Jingzi lifted voices — Golden Melody Award, traditional Amis music. Two generations of the same Malan tribe bloodline, two entirely different art forms of excellence.
