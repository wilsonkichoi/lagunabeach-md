# Korean (ko) Translation Research for Taiwan.md

_Research date: 2026-05-24 · Trigger: Korean professional translator at last Friday's talk callout that Taiwan.md's ko translation "isn't the standard professional choice." Current site state: **대만 14,740 hits vs 타이완 4,427 hits** (~3.3× imbalance favoring the colloquial/government form over the NIKL-recommended form)._

---

## 1. TL;DR (5 bullets)

1. **타이완 is the NIKL (국립국어원) recommended form. 대만 is the South Korean government default and reader-comfort default. Korean media uses both inconsistently — often even within a single article.** This is genuinely contested terrain, not a settled rule. The translator's callout is real: a sovereignty-respecting **academic / encyclopedic register treats 타이완 as primary**, while popular media + ROK government use 대만.

2. **For Taiwan.md, the sovereignty-preservation philosophy points to 타이완 as primary headword in body prose, with 대만 acceptable in informal contexts and as a secondary/parenthetical form**. Korean Encyclopedia of Korean Culture (한국민족문화대백과사전), Korean Wikipedia's NIKL transliteration guideline, and 국립국어원 official rules all confirm 타이완 as the principal form. This brings Taiwan.md/ko into alignment with the **same logic that gives us 베이징 (not 북경), 상하이 (not 상해), 도쿄 (not 동경)** — the modern self-naming respected over inherited Sino-Korean readings.

3. **The "타이완 = PRC-coded, 대만 = sovereignty-affirming" framing from a 2012 SBS column is a minority/inverted reading.** The dominant scholarly position is the opposite: 타이완 respects Taiwan's own Mandarin self-naming (and the post-1911 Xinhai Revolution NIKL rule); 대만 is simply Sino-Korean inertia analogous to 북경/동경. Neither form is overtly PRC-coded, but the **`중국 대만` / `중국 타이베이` constructions ARE PRC-coded** and must be avoided.

4. **Personal name rule is unambiguous: post-1911 Xinhai Revolution figures use Pinyin-based Korean transliteration (차이잉원, 마잉주, 라이칭더, 리덩후이)**. Pre-1911 historical figures (鄭成功 → 정성공) use Sino-Korean readings. This is NIKL 외래어 표기법 §4 Ch.2, codified 1986. Indigenous names should be transliterated from their **original (Austronesian) pronunciation**, not Mandarin Hanzi readings.

5. **Place name rule has a customary-exception clause**: NIKL prefers Pinyin-based forms (타이베이, 가오슝, 타이중, 타이난, 신주) for all major Taiwanese cities, but allows established Sino-Korean alternates (고웅, 대북) where they have traditional usage. **Taiwan.md should canonicalize on the Pinyin-Korean forms uniformly** — they match what Korean readers will see in news, travel content, and academic writing.

## 2. Section answers with citations

### A. Country/region naming conventions

#### A1. 대만 vs 타이완 vs 중화민국

**The NIKL position (definitive):** 외래어 표기법 includes Taiwan in the list of customarily-permitted dual readings — both `타이완` (Mandarin transliteration) and `대만` (Sino-Korean reading) are acceptable, parallel to `베이징/북경`, `상하이/상해`, `타이베이/대북`. However, 국립국어원 explicitly **recommends 타이완** as the preferred form under the modern (post-1911 Xinhai Revolution) Chinese transliteration rule. [Korean Wikipedia: 중국어의 한글 표기](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/%EC%A4%91%EA%B5%AD%EC%96%B4%EC%9D%98_%ED%95%9C%EA%B8%80_%ED%91%9C%EA%B8%B0); [namu.wiki: 대만](https://namu.wiki/w/%EB%8C%80%EB%A7%8C); [SBS 취재파일: 대만인가? 타이완인가?](https://news.sbs.co.kr/news/endPage.do?news_id=N1001663087).

**The South Korean government position:** MOFA (외교부) country profile is **titled `대만 臺灣(Taiwan)`**, not `타이완`. The Korean government has used 대만 since around the 1988 Seoul Olympics rapprochement with the PRC. [MOFA 국가/지역 정보 (대만)](https://www.mofa.go.kr/www/nation/m_3458/view.do?seq=2&titleNm=%EA%B5%AD%EA%B0%80%EC%A0%95%EB%B3%B4%28%EB%8C%80%EB%A7%8C%29).

**Korean Encyclopedia of Korean Culture (한국민족문화대백과사전):** Uses **타이완 as the primary headword**, with 대만 and 중화민국 listed as alternates (이칭). Notes that the official self-name is 중화민국 (ROC), with 중화 타이베이 used in international organizations. [한국민족문화대백과사전: 타이완](https://encykorea.aks.ac.kr/Article/E0067578).

**Korean Wikipedia:** Uses **중화민국 as article title** (formal state name), with intro stating "현재는 중화민국 대신 국내외에서 영토 중 가장 큰 섬의 이름인 **타이완**(**대만**)이라는 명칭을 주로 사용" — gives 타이완 first, 대만 in parentheses. [Korean Wikipedia: 중화민국](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/%EC%A4%91%ED%99%94%EB%AF%BC%EA%B5%AD).

**Major Korean media:** SBS investigative column (2012-era piece, still cited as the canonical analysis) explicitly notes: "국내 언론사들의 표기 방법은 제각각 다릅니다 … 같은 언론사에서도 제목은 '대만'으로, 기사 본문에서는 '타이완'으로 씁니다." Korean media is **systematically inconsistent**; many outlets put 대만 in headlines (because shorter + reader-familiar) and 타이완 in body text. [SBS 취재파일](https://news.sbs.co.kr/news/endPage.do?news_id=N1001663087).

**Progressive/conservative split:** No clean ideological split. Conservative 조선일보 and progressive 한겨레 both use 대만 as their predominant default in headlines. The 타이완 form is more visible in academic writing, encyclopedic content, recent travel/culture journalism, and 통신사 (Yonhap) wire-service style.

**Has the convention shifted in the past decade?** Yes, gradually toward 타이완 in encyclopedic and academic contexts, driven by (a) NIKL's continuing recommendation, (b) the broader Pinyin-transliteration shift that gave us 베이징, 상하이, 광저우 replacing the older Sino-Korean forms, and (c) Korean readers' rising exposure to Taiwan as a distinct cultural/political entity post-2016 (Tsai era, Hong Kong protests, semiconductor focus, cross-Strait tension).

**Korean Taiwan-studies academics:** 한국대만학회 and KCI-indexed Taiwan-studies papers use **타이완** predominantly as the standard academic form. Encyclopedia of Korean Culture confirms this convention. [한국민족문화대백과사전: 타이완](https://encykorea.aks.ac.kr/Article/E0067578); [DBpia: 2.28사건 연구](https://www.dbpia.co.kr/journal/articleDetail?nodeId=NODE08749658).

**Is the 대만/타이완 choice politically loaded?** **Yes, but in the OPPOSITE direction from what SBS suggested.** The 2012 SBS column argues that 타이완 (Mandarin pronunciation) "타이완이라고 부르면 그것은 타이완이 별도의 독립국가가 아니라 중화인민공화국 내의 한 지역을 뜻합니다" — but this framing is contested:

- The dominant academic and encyclopedic reading is the inverse: **타이완 respects Taiwan's own self-naming in its dominant language (Mandarin)**, just as 도쿄 respects Japan's self-naming and 베이징 respects the PRC's. 대만 is a Sino-Korean reading inherited from when Korea referred to Taiwan through Classical Chinese characters, with no implicit sovereignty stance.
- The actual PRC-coded constructions are `중국 대만` / `중국 타이베이` (the form China demands for Taiwanese artists/athletes in PRC-made dramas and on PRC platforms), not the bare 대만 or 타이완.

**Conclusion for Taiwan.md/ko:** Use **타이완 as primary**. The 14,740 vs 4,427 imbalance is genuinely below standard professional convention for the academic/encyclopedic register Taiwan.md occupies. _The Korean translator's callout is correct._

#### A2. Official name (중화민국, ROC, etc.)

- **중화민국** is the official constitutional name. Use when discussing the constitutional/legal entity, founding history, ROC institutions (e.g., 중화민국 총통, 중화민국 헌법, 중화민국 외교부).
- **ROC** as a Latin acronym should be glossed at first use: `중화민국(ROC, Republic of China)`. Don't use bare "ROC" without context.
- **자유중국** is a Cold War-era term (Korea used it until ROK-PRC normalization in 1992); **avoid in modern context** except when historically appropriate. [v.daum article 2025-10-18](https://v.daum.net/v/20251018161012261).
- **대만/타이완 정부** (Taiwan government) is appropriate for current-affairs writing where the constitutional name is unwieldy.

### B. Personal name romanization

#### B1. Pinyin vs Sino-Korean — the 1911 cutoff

NIKL 외래어 표기법 §4 제2장 (동양의 인명·지명 표기) establishes the **신해혁명 (1911) cutoff**:

> "중국 인명은 과거인과 현대인을 구분하여 과거인은 종전의 한자음대로 표기하고, 현대인은 원칙적으로 중국어 표기법에 따라 표기하되, 필요한 경우 한자를 병기한다."

> ([NIKL 외래어 표기법 제4장](https://www.korean.go.kr/front/page/pageView.do?page_id=P000146&mn_id=97); [namu.wiki: 중국어 한글 표기 논쟁](https://namu.wiki/w/%EC%A4%91%EA%B5%AD%EC%96%B4%20%ED%95%9C%EA%B8%80%20%ED%91%9C%EA%B8%B0%20%EB%85%BC%EC%9F%81))

**Rule:** 신해혁명 (1911) is the dividing line, established by NIKL in 1986.

- **Pre-1911**: Korean Sino-Korean reading (정성공 for 鄭成功, 임칙서 for 林則徐, 손중산 for 孫中山 — though 孫中山 is borderline and often given as 쑨원/孫文)
- **Post-1911**: Pinyin-based Korean transliteration (차이잉원, 마잉주, 라이칭더, 리덩후이, 천수이볜, 장제스, 장징궈)

**Pre-1911 figures with established Sino-Korean readings:**

- 鄭成功 → 정성공 (NEVER 정청궁; deeply established as 정성공 in Korean historiography)
- 孫文/孫中山 → 손문/손중산 (older) or **쑨원/쑨중산** (modern, post-Pinyin shift). Both seen; trend toward 쑨원.
- 蔣中正 → 장중정 (rare) — modern use is **장제스** (蔣介石, Chiang Kai-shek) since Chiang is post-1911 and Pinyin-rule applies.

#### B2. Exceptions for transitional figures

**李登輝 (Lee Teng-hui) is the canonical edge case.** Born 1923 (post-1911), so NIKL rule says Pinyin → 리덩후이. But Korean media long used 이등휘 (Sino-Korean reading) — Wikipedia headword is **리덩후이** now ([Korean Wikipedia: 리덩후이](https://ko.wikipedia.org/wiki/%EB%A6%AC%EB%8D%A9%ED%9B%84%EC%9D%B4)). Use **리덩후이** as primary, with `(이등휘)` glossed on first mention in historical/long-form contexts where readers may have encountered the older form.

**蔣介石 (Chiang Kai-shek):**

- Korean: **장제스** is overwhelmingly dominant (Pinyin-based)
- The Sino-Korean 장개석 is older / partially deprecated
- Note: Korean media sometimes uses 장개석 still in conservative outlets; 장제스 is the modern academic standard

**蔣經國 (Chiang Ching-kuo) → 장징궈** (modern standard).

#### B3. Indigenous, Hokkien, Hakka, Aboriginal names

**Mainstream Korean convention transliterates from Mandarin Hanzi reading**, which is problematic for sovereignty-respect. Better practice (and Taiwan.md should adopt this):

- **Indigenous (Austronesian) personal names**: Transliterate from the **original Austronesian pronunciation**, not Mandarin Hanzi readings. e.g., Iwan Nawi → 이완 나위, not 衣灣那威 → 이완나위(?).
- **Indigenous people groups**: Pinyin-Korean is the namu.wiki/Wiki standard (아메이족 for 阿美族/Amis, 파이완족 for Paiwan, 부눙족 for Bunun, 타이야족 for Atayal, 야미족/타오족 for Yami/Tao). [namu.wiki: 대만 원주민](https://namu.wiki/w/%EB%8C%80%EB%A7%8C%20%EC%9B%90%EC%A3%BC%EB%AF%BC).
- **Hokkien/Hakka personal names**: For modern public figures, **follow how the person self-romanizes** (e.g., if a person uses their Hokkien name in English-language press, mirror in Korean). For historical figures, Pinyin-from-Mandarin is the default unless a Hokkien/Hakka form is documented.

#### B4. Established Korean-media names

- 安溥 / 張懸 (Deserts Chang → Anpu) — Korean media uses **장쉬안** (former stage name, from Pinyin Zhāng Xuán) and **안푸** (current name, from Pinyin Ānpǔ). NOT 안서경 (this would be the Sino-Korean reading of 安溥). Verify case-by-case for K-pop/J-pop comparables and music critics' usage.
- 周杰倫 (Jay Chou) → 저우제룬
- 王力宏 (Wang Leehom) → 왕리훙
- 鄧麗君 (Teresa Teng) → 덩리쥔 (NOT 등려군, which is the older Sino-Korean form occasionally seen)
- 林志玲 (Lin Chi-ling) → 린즈링

### F. Honorifics and register

#### F1. Sentence-ending style

**Recommendation for Taiwan.md/ko: 해라체 (declarative `한다` / `이다` / `이었다`)** — this is the standard for encyclopedic, journalistic, and academic prose in Korean. Korean Wikipedia uses 해라체 for article bodies (with 하십시오체 only for instructions like "보십시오"). Used in newspapers, academic papers, explanatory writing — matches Taiwan.md's tone register. [namu.wiki: 해라체](https://namu.wiki/w/%ED%95%B4%EB%9D%BC%EC%B2%B4).

**Avoid:**

- 합니다체 / 하십시오체 — too formal/instructional; reads as ad copy or government announcement
- 해체 / 해요체 — too informal; reads as blog post
- Mixing styles within an article — strict consistency required

#### F2. Collective voice (我 / 我們)

Chinese-source `我` / `我們` becomes problematic in Korean translation. Options:

- **Best**: Recast in third person ("타이완 사회는…", "대만인들은…", "이 글에서는…") — matches encyclopedic register.
- **Acceptable**: Drop entirely (Korean tolerates subject omission better than English).
- **Avoid**: `우리 (we)` — implies the writer shares ethnic/national identity with reader. Inappropriate for a public knowledge platform with multi-cultural readership.
- **Avoid**: `필자 (this author)` — too formal/academic for general-readership prose.

#### F3. Taiwanese (台語/Hokkien) loanwords in zh-TW source

When Mandarin source includes Hokkien-origin terms in quotation or stylization:

- Transliterate the Hokkien pronunciation in Hangul (e.g., 阿公 → 아공/akong; 阿嬤 → 아마/ama)
- Provide Hanzi + Korean gloss: `아공(阿公, 대만어로 할아버지)`
- For dish names, use the Hokkien transliteration if that's how the dish is internationally known (어아젠 for 蚵仔煎)
- Don't auto-convert Hokkien terms to Mandarin Pinyin readings — preserves source-language fidelity

#### G3. Korean-language critique of PRC-coded framing

The clearest available Korean-language argument is the Pressian/Daum article "대만도 중국어 쓴다? 한국 사람이 한국어 쓰는 것과 의미는 다르다" (2025-12), which argues that saying "Taiwanese speak Chinese" misrepresents linguistic reality and implicitly imports PRC framing. The piece documents Taiwan's multilingualism (Mandarin, Taiwanese/Min Nan ~86%, Hakka ~13%, indigenous languages) and traces how Mandarin became dominant only after 1945 ROC enforcement. ([Pressian](https://www.pressian.com/pages/articles/2025120515271492321); [v.daum 2025-12-06](https://v.daum.net/v/20251206170452210)). Use as a model for sovereignty-aware Korean prose.

## 5. Stylistic register recommendations

- **Sentence-ending style**: 해라체 (`한다`, `이다`, `이었다`, `있다`, `없다`) for all article body content. Strict consistency — never mix with 합니다체 or 해요체 within a single article. Aligns with Korean Wikipedia, encyclopedic prose, and quality Korean journalism.

- **Headline style**: Korean media convention is shorter+punchier than body. Acceptable to use 대만 in headlines where character economy matters AND body uses 타이완 (matches actual Korean media practice).

- **First-person collective**: Avoid `우리` (we). Recast in third person: `대만 사회는...`, `대만인들은...`, `이 글에서는...`. Drop subject when Korean grammar permits.

- **Loanword density**: Pinyin-transliterated proper nouns are fine, but **gloss Hanzi on first mention** for any specialized term: `루러우판(滷肉飯)`, `펑리수(鳳梨酥)`, `2·28 사건(二二八事件)`. Second mention onward, Korean-only.

- **Numbers + dates**: Convert all 民國 dates to Gregorian on Taiwan.md, formatted as `2024년(민국 113년)` on first mention if民國 is contextually meaningful (e.g., quoting a ROC document), otherwise just `2024년`.

- **Currency**: 신타이완 달러 (TWD) / 대만 달러 / NTD. First mention spell out, then 달러 or NT$.

- **Mixed-script policy**: Hanzi parenthetical glosses are encouraged for political/historical/cultural terms where the Hanzi clarifies meaning (`본성인(本省人)`, `국부천대(國府遷臺)`, `백색 테러(白色恐怖)`). Don't use Hanzi when Korean reading is fully naturalized (no need to gloss `중국`, `일본`, `대만`).

- **Quotation handling**: Direct quotes from Mandarin sources — translate to natural Korean, then optionally include original Mandarin in parens or footnote. Don't transliterate spoken Mandarin word-by-word.

- **Linking convention**: When name has both Pinyin-Korean and Sino-Korean forms in circulation, primary form is the article title; alternate forms become redirects. e.g., `리덩후이` is primary, `이등휘` is redirect.

3. **한국대만학회 official style guide**: The Korean Association for Taiwan Studies likely has a journal style guide for `대만연구` submissions; couldn't fetch directly. Recommend reaching out for official confirmation.

4. **Specific city naming edge cases**:
   - 嘉義 (자이) — confirmed Pinyin form, but some Korean sources use 가의 (Sino-Korean); usage trend should be re-verified for travel content.
   - 苗栗 (먀오리) — also seen as 먀오리현/먀오리시; township-level transliteration policy may need finalization.

5. **Personal name borderline cases pre/post-1911**:
   - 林獻堂 (1881-1956) — straddles 1911. Modern academic usage = 린셴탕 (Pinyin); older usage = 임헌당. Recommend 린셴탕.
   - 蔣渭水 (1891-1931) — same. Recommend 장웨이수이.
   - Recommendation: For figures born pre-1911 but active post-1911, default to Pinyin unless there's deep Korean-historiography prior usage.

6. **Indigenous group names — language source**:
   - Current Korean convention transliterates from Mandarin Hanzi reading (e.g., 阿美族 → 아메이족). Best-practice from indigenous-respect angle would be to transliterate from Austronesian endonym (e.g., Pangcah → 팡차흐). Korean precedent for endonym-based transliteration exists but isn't universal. Taiwan.md should choose a policy explicitly.

7. **Hokkien food name transliteration consistency**: 蚵仔煎 transliterated from Hokkien (어아젠) while 滷肉飯 transliterated from Mandarin (루러우판). Korean convention is mixed; recommend documenting per-dish rationale.

8. **Religious figures in Taiwan-Buddhist context**: 證嚴法師 (Cheng Yen), 星雲大師 (Hsing Yun), 聖嚴法師 (Sheng Yen) — Pinyin-Korean is modern standard but older Korean Buddhist literature may use Sino-Korean forms. Per-figure verification needed.
