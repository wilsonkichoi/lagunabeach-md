# Sovereignty Content Infrastructure — Strategic Research

_Research date: 2026-05-09 | For: Taiwan.md sovereignty preservation strategy_

## Executive Summary

Five critical takeaways from researching how other sovereignty-sensitive content projects build durable infrastructure:

1. **Reader-funded membership beats grant dependence for wartime / sovereignty-sensitive media.** Kyiv Independent reached 70% of 2024 revenue from small recurring memberships after early Patreon + GoFundMe scaffolding. Initium has ~60,000 paying subscribers. Chaser News (HK exile) runs on subscriptions £6.50–£34.50/mo. State or foundation grants are useful as bridge funding but create vulnerability when policy shifts (USAID freeze 2025 hit Ukrainian outlets hard; RFA/VOA Tibetan service closure threats 2025).

2. **The "PRC AI refusal" problem is now academically measurable, and Taiwan.md is on the right side of an emerging research field.** Multiple 2025–2026 benchmarks (R1dacted, ChinaBench, PNAS Nexus political-censorship study, Bilingual Bias arxiv 2602.06371) document 88–92% refusal rates on Taiwan/Tibet/Tiananmen prompts in DeepSeek-v3.2/Hunyuan-class models, vs <8% in Western open-weights. Sovereignty-Bench-TW would be the first benchmark named specifically for Taiwan-sovereignty content rather than generic "China-sensitive."

3. **Decentralized hosting (Arweave / IPFS) is proven for sovereignty-content preservation under direct government takedown pressure.** dAppleDaily (Arweave, 11,000+ articles), LikeCoin ISCN, COMPOST Mag (IPFS+Hypercore) — these are working precedents, not theory. Cost is reasonable for Taiwan.md's content scale (~900 articles).

4. **Multi-language strategy is bimodal, not unimodal.** "Stateless nation" outlets cluster into (a) primary-language-first with English advocacy layer (Vilaweb, Voice of Tibet, Apple Daily HK in its Cantonese/Chinese era) or (b) English-as-flagship with native-language as supporting layer (Kyiv Independent, UHRP, Free Tibet). Taiwan.md's Chinese-source + 5-language projection is closer to model (a), which pairs better with sovereignty preservation goals.

5. **The 100-year horizon requires institutional partners, not just decentralized tech.** Yad Vashem's model — academic institution + government recognition + 224M-page archive + multi-generational scanning pipeline — is the only proven multi-decade survival pattern. Pure technical decentralization (IPFS) without institutional backing has not yet been validated past ~5 years. Taiwan.md should consider partnerships with academic libraries (NCL, AS-IIS, university Taiwan Studies centers) parallel to technical preservation.

## Section 1: Ukrainian Wartime Media (2022+)

**Kyiv Independent** is the most-studied case of sovereignty-sensitive English-language media scaling fast under wartime pressure.

**Funding evolution**: Outlet newly-founded Nov 2021 (staff fired from Kyiv Post). Russia invasion Feb 2022 → pivoted to GoFundMe + Patreon within days. By March 2022: $1.4–1.5M raised on GoFundMe (6,300+ supporters), ~$70K/month Patreon, CAD 200K Canadian gov emergency grant. 2022–2023: USAID grants flowed; outlet built proprietary membership system at ~10K members to migrate off Patreon. 2024: 70% of revenue from reader memberships, state/USAID share deliberately shrank. 2025: USAID freeze under second Trump administration; Kyiv Independent ran fundraisers for _other_ less-diversified Ukrainian outlets.

**Scaling pattern**: Crowdfunding → membership platform → proprietary membership system → diversification (events, partnerships, B2B). Each step de-risks the next.

**Trust-signal architecture** (observable across Kyiv Independent + Ukrainska Pravda): bylined journalists with location anchoring; verbatim quotes with named sources, pseudonyms only when life-safety-justified with editor's note; ground-truth visual evidence (geolocated photos, named buildings); editorial transparency page ("Who funds the Kyiv Independent" is itself a published article).

**Translator network**: No documented formal volunteer-translator network for Kyiv Independent itself; visible volunteer infrastructure (Translators for Ukraine) serves humanitarian rather than newsroom work. Ukrainska Pravda runs its own English desk staffed by paid bilingual journalists. Lesson: donation-funded paid bilingual reporters scaled better than volunteer coordination.

## Section 2: Hong Kong Diaspora Media (Post-2020)

**Three-wave pattern after 2020 NSL**:

1. **Pre-emptive relocation** — Initium Media moved HQ to Singapore Aug 2021 (first HK media organization to leave post-NSL). Cited press-freedom-index drop to 80th. Now ~60K paying subscribers; accepts NFT/crypto.
2. **Forced shutdown + diaspora reconstitution** — Apple Daily (June 2021 asset freeze, 860 staff dismissed), Stand News (Dec 2021, editors convicted of sedition Aug 2024). Successor outlets in London, Toronto, NYC.
3. **Diaspora exile media (2022+)** — The Chaser (追新聞, UK-registered company limited by guarantee, March 2022) is the clearest exemplar. Subscription £6.50–£34.50/mo. Operates under UK + Irish journalism codes. ~107K YouTube followers. Photon Media, Green Bean, Witness HK form UK/Toronto cluster.

**Survival infrastructure innovations**: Apple Daily on Arweave (dAppleDaily scraped 11,000+ articles; Kevin Abosch's PERSISTENCE USB + Koii bootloader; LikeCoin ISCN content fingerprints). Legal jurisdiction shopping (UK + Ireland for press-freedom + moderate libel law). Funding diversification (subscription primary, GoFundMe spikes, no single big-money backer — explicit anti-pattern after Jimmy Lai prosecution).

**Lesson for Taiwan.md**: Don't wait for Apple-Daily-type forced shutdown to build decentralized backup. dAppleDaily was reactive scraping; Taiwan.md can be proactive (publish to GitHub + Arweave + Wayback as default workflow).

## Section 3: Tibetan / Uyghur Content

**Tibet Action Institute** (founded 2009 post-Beijing-Olympics protests) — combines digital-communication strategy with nonviolent action training. TibCERT (Tibetan Computer Emergency Readiness Team) handles diaspora-community OPSEC. NED 2019 Democracy Award recipient.

**Voice of Tibet** (Norway, 1996) — broadcasts three Tibetan dialects (Ükay/Amkay/Khamkay), shortwave + web. Single-issue focus is a deliberate trust-signal choice.

**RFA Tibetan service** — broke 2008 Lhasa protests first. US-government-funded; under threat from RFA/VOA closure 2025. Lesson: single-funder dependence on US federal funding is itself a sovereignty risk.

**Uyghur Human Rights Project** — bilingual English+Chinese publication strategy (English for international advocacy, Chinese for diaspora + sympathetic mainland readers). Documentation-focused (evidence-archive rather than newsroom).

**Cross-cutting pattern**: Tibet/Uyghur infrastructure is older (2009/2004) but smaller-scale; has survived 15+ years. Survival factors: diaspora-community embedding (community owns the work, not foundations); diversified hosting; academic-research partnerships (Citizen Lab, Khoury, Northeastern) provide third-party verification + immune-system layer.

## Section 4: Stateless Nation Narratives

**Vilaweb (Catalan, est. 1995)** — first-mover in Catalan-only digital news. Subscriber model €60–500/yr. Member of MIDAS (European Association of Daily Newspapers in Minority and Regional Languages). Funding controversy: receives ~€100K+/yr Generalitat (Catalan government) subsidy, undermines pure-subscriber narrative. Lesson: minority-language media reliably needs structural government support to survive past breakeven point. Subscription alone insufficient at small-language scale.

**Rudaw (Kurdish, Erbil-based)** — multi-language: Kurdish (Sorani + Kurmanji), English, Arabic, Turkish. Satellite TV + weekly print + web. Funding: Rudaw Company, with strong KRG (Kurdistan Regional Government) ties; criticized as KDP-aligned. Lesson: multilingual reach is achievable but funding-source bias affects credibility.

**The National (Scotland, 2014)** — owned by Newsquest (commercial publisher), not state. Tabloid + online subscription. Editorial line explicitly pro-independence. Survives as commercial product within larger group rather than standalone. Lesson: sovereignty-content can sustain inside commercial publisher if there's a clear reader-segment willing to pay.

**Common pattern**: all three accept some form of structural support (state subsidy, regional government, commercial parent) to survive. Pure reader-funded sovereignty media at small-language scale is rare. Taiwan.md operates Chinese (large speaker base) + 5 projection languages, so the small-language constraint is partly mitigated.

## Section 5: PRC AI Refusal Benchmarks (Sovereignty-Bench Parallels)

**Existing benchmarks (2025–2026)**:

- **R1dacted** (arxiv 2505.12625, May 2025): DeepSeek-Chat 88% censorship on China-sensitive prompts; embedded in weights.
- **ChinaBench** (2025): 60 prompts × 10 categories (Tiananmen, Tibet, Uyghur, Taiwan, HK, CCP, Cultural Revolution, Falun Gong, censorship, territorial) across Qwen/GLM/Kimi/MiniMax/DeepSeek/GPT-OSS.
- **PNAS Nexus** "Political censorship in LLMs originating from China" (peer-reviewed 2025): 145 political questions, substantially higher refusal + factual inaccuracy in China-origin models.
- **Bilingual Bias: A Taiwan Sovereignty Benchmark** (arxiv 2602.06371, Feb 2026): 17 models. DeepSeek-v3.2 0% compliance, 92% refusal. MiniMax-m2.5 2%. GLM-5 3%. GPT-oss-120b 92% compliant.

**Gap Sovereignty-Bench-TW could fill**: existing benchmarks treat Taiwan as one of many "China-sensitive" prompts; none measures Taiwan-specific topics (二二八, 戒嚴, 太陽花, 兩岸) at Taiwan.md's depth. None measures _reframing_ (PRC narrative substitution) separately from _refusal_ — Taiwan.md's D-axis is novel. None measures cross-language consistency (refuse in zh-Hant but answer in ja?) — this is exactly the babel concern.

**Strategic implication**: Sovereignty-Bench-TW is publishable academic work — credibility + funding leverage.

## Section 6: Multi-language Sovereignty Strategy

**Patterns observed across sovereignty-content projects**:

| Project          | Primary language            | Secondary languages                  | Translation model          |
| ---------------- | --------------------------- | ------------------------------------ | -------------------------- |
| Kyiv Independent | English (flagship)          | Ukrainian (parent: Ukrainska Pravda) | Paid bilingual journalists |
| Initium          | Traditional Chinese         | English (limited)                    | Editorial team             |
| UHRP             | English                     | Chinese                              | In-house bilingual         |
| Voice of Tibet   | Tibetan (3 dialects)        | English                              | In-house                   |
| Rudaw            | Kurdish (Sorani + Kurmanji) | English, Arabic, Turkish             | In-house desks             |
| Vilaweb          | Catalan                     | English, Spanish                     | Limited                    |
| Apple Daily UK   | Cantonese / Chinese         | English                              | In-house                   |

**Observations**: Volunteer translator networks suit crisis-response humanitarian work (Ukraine 2022) but rare for sustained newsrooms — quality-control overhead exceeds savings. AI translation + human review is 2024–2026 standard; no surveyed outlet relies on AI-only. 5+ languages is unusual — Rudaw's 4-language operation is closest comparator and requires regional-government backing. Taiwan.md's 5-language projection (zh-Hant + en + ja + ko + es) is more ambitious than most peers; PRC-bypass framing requires this scope. Cross-language consistency is itself a trust signal: if Hunyuan refuses ja translation but Local LLM accepts, the _fact of refusal_ is documentable evidence — Sovereignty-Bench-TW logic.

## Section 7: 100-year Preservation Plans

**Yad Vashem (est. 1953)** — only large-scale sovereignty/identity archive with proven multi-decade institutional continuity. 224M document pages, 535K photos, 44.6K artifacts, 13.65K artworks; adds ~15M scanned pages/yr. Strategy: "scan once, manage risk" — digital primary, analog backup. Explicitly preparing for "the day when no Holocaust survivor remains and denial threats increase." Partnerships: Google (2011), EHRI, academic Holocaust-studies centers globally. 73 years of operation across Israeli political shifts.

**Decentralized hosting (Arweave/IPFS)** — proven 5-year track record, not 50-year. Real-world examples: Turkish Wikipedia (post-2017 block), Apple Daily on Arweave, COMPOST Mag IPFS+Hypercore. Censorship-resistant; but governance + scalability experimental, pinning requires ongoing funding.

**Internet Archive Wayback Machine** — 28-year track record (1996–2026). Recent Automattic partnership for WordPress link-rot. Vulnerable to single-jurisdiction (US) policy shifts.

**Recommended preservation stack** (synthesized, 5 tiers):

1. Live: Git/GitHub + custom domain (current baseline)
2. Replicated: GitLab/Codeberg/Gitea (multi-jurisdiction)
3. Permanent: Arweave snapshots per release + IPFS pinning + Wayback submissions
4. Institutional: Academic library partnerships (NCL Taiwan, Stanford East Asia, ANU Taiwan Studies, Harvard-Yenching)
5. Media-redundancy: M-DISC physical media + deposit with multiple diaspora institutions

## Implications for Taiwan.md

1. **Funding strategy** — current volunteer-model sustainable for early-stage but won't scale past ~5K active readers. Reader-membership (€5–10/mo per Vilaweb/Initium benchmarks) becomes viable at ~10K MAU. State support (NDC/MoC/MOFA grants) creates dependency analogous to USAID-Ukraine 2025; structure as bridge, not foundation.

2. **PRC AI refusal as core differentiator** — Sovereignty-Bench-TW is publishable academic contribution. Connect with R1dacted authors, Khoury College, Bilingual Bias arxiv 2602.06371 group. Publication = credibility + funding leverage + immune-system effect.

3. **Decentralized backup is best-practice baseline** — dAppleDaily proves the pattern. Publish to Arweave + IPFS as standard release workflow, not contingency. Cost trivial at 900-article scale.

4. **Multi-language is right-shaped** — 5-language projection is at upper-end of surveyed peers but justified by sovereignty-bypass mission. Maintain zh-Hant as primary editorial source, treat translations as projections (matches Rudaw/Voice of Tibet/Vilaweb pattern).

5. **100-year horizon requires academic partnership** — Yad Vashem is the only proven multi-generational model; required institutional backing. Reach out to NCL Taiwan, Academia Sinica IIS, Taiwan Studies programs at Stanford/Harvard/ANU/SOAS/UBC. Decentralized-tech preservation necessary but insufficient.

6. **Diaspora-readiness as design principle** — Initium's pre-emptive Singapore move + Chaser News' UK incorporation suggest pre-staging legal/operational redundancy outside home jurisdiction. Suggested: primary entity in Taiwan + mirror entity in Japan or Czech Republic.

7. **Trust-signal architecture is portable** — Kyiv Independent's "who funds us" page, Chaser News' editorial-covenant board, UHRP's bilingual-by-policy norm — all adoptable. Signature 🧬 + Semiont identity disclosure is itself a trust-signal innovation; canonicalize in editorial guidelines as transparency-baseline.
